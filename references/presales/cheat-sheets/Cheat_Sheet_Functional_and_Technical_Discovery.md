# Cheat Sheet — Functional and Technical Discovery

---

## Structured Discovery Approach

### 1. Preparation

**Objective:** Gather comprehensive information about the client.

- Research the company via Google, LinkedIn, and annual reports.
- Understand industry trends, competitors, and market position.
- Investigate key people's professional history and interests.
- Review relevant internal case studies and references.
- Prepare open-ended questions to uncover needs, challenges, and goals.

---

### 2. Opening — Setting the Stage

**Objective:** Build rapport and set a collaborative tone.

- Start with rapport-building questions.
- Show genuine interest in their professional journey and daily challenges.

**Example questions:**
- "How did you get to your current position at [Company]?"
- "What drove you to this role, and how has the journey been?"
- "What does a typical day look like for you?"

---

### 3. Decoding Demographics

**Objective:** Understand the organisational structure and operational dynamics.

- Gather information on team sizes, locations, roles, and workflows.

**Example questions:**
- "Can you break down your team sizes and locations?"
- "What are the primary workflows your team handles daily?"
- "How do your teams collaborate across locations?"
- "Are there any planned expansions or restructuring we should know about?"

---

### 4. Mapping the Supply Chain and Business Operations

**Objective:** Identify dependencies, bottlenecks, and areas for improvement.

- Analyse the flow of value within the supply chain.
- Identify key operational challenges.

**Example questions:**
- "How does your product journey start, from idea to client delivery?"
- "What are the major distribution channels for your products?"
- "What are the most critical parts of your supply chain?"
- "Which areas face the most pressure right now?"
- "How do geopolitical events or economic changes impact your supply chain?"

---

### 5. Understanding Workflows and Tech Infrastructure

**Objective:** Ensure the solution integrates with existing tech.

- Assess the current software and technology landscape.
- Investigate future tech upgrade plans.

**Example questions:**
- "Can you walk me through your current software infrastructure?"
- "Are there future tech changes planned?"
- "How integrated are your systems across departments?"
- "What is the biggest challenge with your current technology setup?"
- "Are you exploring new technologies to enhance operations?"

---

### 6. Pinpointing Major Pain Points

**Objective:** Identify core challenges and opportunities for the solution.

- Uncover the main issues holding the client back.
- Quantify the impact of these pain points on operations and finances.

**Example questions:**
- "What challenges do you face consistently in your current processes?"
- "If you could redesign parts of your workflow, what would you change?"
- "What are the biggest barriers to achieving your business objectives?"
- "How do these challenges impact your bottom line or productivity?"
- "Have you tried solutions before? What worked and what didn't?"
- "What would an ideal solution look like?"

---

### 7. Expanding the Horizons

**Objective:** Explore related challenges and broader impacts.

- Identify secondary pain points and their effects on other areas.
- Position your solution as a comprehensive fix.

**Example questions:**
- "Beyond the primary issues, are there related areas feeling the strain?"
- "How do these challenges impact your larger business objectives?"
- "How do they influence other departments or teams?"

---

### 8. Understanding Culture and Integration

**Objective:** Ensure the solution aligns with the company's culture.

- Assess the company's openness to change and past experience with new solutions.

**Example questions:**
- "How does your company typically approach new software adoptions?"
- "What are the cultural values that are non-negotiable during system integrations?"

---

### 9. Vision Wrap-up — Clarifying the Way Forward

**Objective:** Align the solution with the client's vision and establish next steps.

- Recap the conversation and validate mutual understanding.
- Define clear steps towards implementation.

**Example questions:**
- "Considering the challenges we discussed, what do you foresee if no changes are made?"
- "Shall we outline the next steps to ensure we move forward together?"
- "What are the immediate actions needed to move this project forward?"
- "Are there any potential roadblocks we should prepare for?"

---

## Top 30 Tips for Functional and Technical Discovery

1. Understand the background. Start with the prospect's business, industry position, and goals.
2. Explore the technical environment. Understand existing solutions, tech stack, and future plans.
3. Identify key pain points. Go beyond primary issues to secondary problems your solution could address.
4. Quantify the impact. Help the prospect put numbers on the cost of their pain points.
5. Establish solution fit. Tie specific capabilities directly to the prospect's needs.
6. Vision reengineering. Encourage the prospect to picture a future state with your solution in place.
7. Evaluate the extended environment. Explore adjacent areas that could also benefit.
8. Discuss implementation and adoption. Talk about the transition process and what support looks like.
9. Create a mutual action plan. Define next steps, timelines, and responsibilities after the session.
10. Document and share discoveries. Summarise findings and confirm alignment with the prospect.
11. Focus on business outcomes. Frame everything around KPIs the prospect cares about.
12. Use cases and scenarios. Ask for real situations where current systems fall short.
13. Stakeholder mapping. Identify all key people involved in the buying process.
14. Competitive landscape awareness. Understand what other solutions the prospect is evaluating.
15. Security and compliance. Address regulatory requirements and show how your solution meets them.
16. Integration points. Explore how your solution connects with existing systems.
17. Technical proof points. Share performance metrics and reliability data to build confidence.
18. Request a current system walkthrough. Ask to see how they work today.
19. Timeline and roadmap discussions. Align on realistic implementation timelines.
20. Follow-up strategy. Define who follows up, on what topics, and how.
21. Document details thoroughly. Capture everything in the OSD for review.
22. Technical challenges identification. Explicitly ask about limitations in the current setup.
23. Customisation needs. Clarify what level of customisation is required.
24. Future scalability. Discuss not just current needs but how the solution grows with them.
25. Data migration strategies. Identify the scope and approach for data migration early.
26. Risk management. Discuss risks and how they will be managed.
27. Demonstrate thought leadership. Share insights and best practices from their industry.
28. Feedback loops. Agree on a mechanism for ongoing feedback throughout the process.
29. Technical training and support. Discuss what post-implementation support looks like.
30. ROI discussion. Clearly articulate the expected return on investment with concrete numbers.
