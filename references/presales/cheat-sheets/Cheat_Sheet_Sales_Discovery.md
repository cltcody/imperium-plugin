# Cheat Sheet — Sales Discovery

---

## Framework Comparison: BANT vs CHAMP vs MEDDIC

| Aspect | BANT | CHAMP | MEDDIC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full Form | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline | Challenges, Authority, Money, Prioritization | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion |
| Focus | Basic qualification | Customer-centric, challenges and priorities | Comprehensive buying process |
| Budget/Cost | Budget | Money | Metrics (ROI and financial impact) |
| Decision Makers | Authority | Authority | Economic Buyer, Champion |
| Needs/Challenges | Need | Challenges | Identify Pain |
| Timeline/Urgency | Timeline | Prioritization | Decision Process, Identify Pain |
| Criteria | — | — | Decision Criteria |
| Process | — | — | Decision Process |
| Internal Advocate | — | — | Champion |
| Key Strength | Simplicity, easy to apply | Understanding and solving customer problems | Detailed, covers all aspects of the buying process |
| Use Case | Quick initial qualification | Medium to complex sales cycles | Complex sales environments |

**When to use which:**
- **BANT** — Good for quick and basic qualification. Focuses on budget, authority, need, and timeline.
- **CHAMP** — More customer-centric. Focuses on understanding the prospect's challenges and priorities.
- **MEDDIC** — Most comprehensive. Ideal for complex sales with detailed decision processes.

## 1. BANT Framework

### Budget
- "What is your allocated budget for this project?"
- "Is there flexibility if the solution exceeds initial expectations?"
- "How do you typically fund similar projects?"
- "Have you previously allocated budget for solving this problem?"
- "What are the budget approval processes in your organisation?"

### Authority
- "Who will be the final decision-maker for this purchase?"
- "Are there other stakeholders involved in the decision?"
- "Who else should be part of this discussion?"
- "How does your organisation make purchasing decisions?"
- "Can you walk me through the decision-making hierarchy?"

### Need
- "What specific challenges are you currently facing?"
- "How critical is it to address this problem in the short term?"
- "What are the key pain points driving this initiative?"
- "How have these issues affected your business performance?"
- "What happens if these needs are not met in the next few months?"

### Timeline
- "When do you need to see results or have the solution implemented?"
- "Are there upcoming events that would accelerate your decision?"
- "What are your target dates for implementation?"
- "How do external factors influence your timeline?"
- "Are there any risks associated with delaying this decision?"

## 2. CHAMP Framework

### Challenges
- "What are the biggest obstacles your team is facing right now?"
- "How are these challenges impacting your business performance?"
- "Have you tried other solutions? What worked and what didn't?"
- "How do these challenges affect your long-term goals?"
- "What internal or external factors contribute to these challenges?"

### Authority
- "Who else needs to be involved to move forward?"
- "What does the decision-making process look like on your end?"
- "Who will champion this project internally?"
- "Can you describe the influence of each stakeholder involved?"
- "What criteria will decision-makers use to evaluate solutions?"

### Money
- "What financial resources have been allocated for this initiative?"
- "How do you justify ROI internally for such investments?"
- "What financial constraints could impact this decision?"
- "Are there specific financial targets or savings you need to achieve?"
- "How do you prioritise spending across departments or projects?"

### Prioritisation
- "Where does solving this issue fall on your priority list?"
- "What are the consequences if this problem stays unaddressed?"
- "What other initiatives are competing for your attention and resources?"
- "How does this project align with your strategic goals?"
- "What would make this project move up or down on your priority list?"

---

## 3. MEDDIC Framework

### Metrics
- "What KPIs are you aiming to improve with this solution?"
- "Can you quantify the expected benefits in terms of cost savings or revenue growth?"
- "What metrics are currently underperforming that this solution could help?"
- "How do you measure the success of similar projects?"
- "What are your target metrics for the next quarter or year?"

### Economic Buyer
- "Who has the final say over budget approval?"
- "What are the economic buyer's main concerns or goals?"
- "How does the economic buyer view the ROI of this project?"
- "What financial metrics are most important to the economic buyer?"
- "Can we schedule a meeting with the economic buyer?"

### Decision Criteria
- "What are the key factors you consider when choosing a solution?"
- "How important are vendor reputation and scalability in your decision?"
- "What specific features or capabilities are must-haves?"
- "Are there any non-negotiables that could disqualify a vendor?"
- "How do you evaluate the long-term value of a solution?"

### Decision Process
- "What steps do you follow to make a purchase decision?"
- "How long does it typically take to finalise a decision?"
- "Who will be involved at each stage of the decision-making process?"
- "Are there any potential roadblocks in your decision-making process?"
- "What can we do to facilitate and speed up your decision?"

### Identify Pain
- "What specific pain points are driving the need for a new solution?"
- "How much is this problem costing you in time or resources?"
- "What are the downstream effects of these pain points on other areas?"
- "How do these pain points compare to other challenges you face?"
- "What solutions have you tried before to address these issues?"

### Champion
- "Is there someone in your organisation advocating for this solution?"
- "How can I support your internal champion in making the case?"
- "What concerns might your champion need to address with other stakeholders?"
- "How influential is your champion in the decision-making process?"
- "What can we provide to empower your champion in their advocacy?"

## When Should PreSales Step into Sales Discovery?

- **Early engagement** — Involve PreSales early when technical insights are needed to set the right expectations.
- **Technical expertise** — Bring in PreSales when detailed technical discussions are needed to navigate complex requirements.

## Top 30 Sales Discovery Tips

1. Start with personal connection. Build rapport through questions about their role and journey.
2. Understand the company and team. Ask about structure, team size, roles, and experience.
3. Explore workflows and processes. Identify inefficiencies and pain points at a high level first.
4. Assess the technical environment. Understand their tech infrastructure and future plans.
5. Identify major pains and related pains. Use visuals like diagrams to uncover primary and secondary issues.
6. Quantify impact and vision reengineering. Discuss measurable impacts and the prospect's ideal future state.
7. Explore the extended environment. Understand the broader business context and adjacent areas.
8. Discuss cultural and organisational uniqueness. Recognise what makes the prospect's company unique.
9. Evaluate the consequences of inaction. Discuss what happens if no solution is implemented.
10. Create a mutual action plan. Conclude with clear next steps that reflect everything learned.
11. Confirm budget early. Understand financial constraints before going deep on solutions.
12. Identify decision makers. Get all key stakeholders involved from the beginning.
13. Assess purchase readiness. Determine how soon they need a solution and what triggers the decision.
14. Explore previous solutions. Find out what has been tried before and why it did or didn't work.
15. Understand compliance and regulations. Ensure your solution meets all necessary requirements.
16. Ask open-ended questions. Encourage detailed responses to get deeper insights.
17. Leverage social proof. Share how similar companies have successfully used your solution.
18. Check competitor influence. Find out what else is being evaluated and why.
19. Seek referrals within the organisation. Ask about other departments that could benefit.
20. Summarise and confirm understanding. Recap key points at the end of every discovery call.
21. Prioritise flexibility in solutions. Highlight how your solution can be tailored and scaled.
22. Emphasise the impact of delay. Discuss costs and missed opportunities from not acting now.
23. Detail the implementation process. Give a clear roadmap with timelines and milestones.
24. Focus on return on investment (ROI). Use data to make the financial case for your solution.
25. Use visual aids. Use diagrams or slides to explain how your solution works or integrates.
26. Handle objections gracefully. Prepare responses to common concerns that educate and reassure.
27. Monitor prospect engagement. Adjust your approach based on how engaged they are.
28. Facilitate internal consensus. Offer materials that help the prospect sell internally.
29. Clarify next steps clearly. Always end each interaction with a clear agreement on what happens next.
30. Leverage technology. Use CRM tools to track insights and personalise every follow-up.
