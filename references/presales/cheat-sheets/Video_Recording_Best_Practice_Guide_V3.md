## The PCV Loop: Your Video Workflow {#the-pcv-loop}

Every video follows three phases: Planning, Creation, and Validation. This is the PCV Loop. Recording sits between heavy strategy work and rigorous quality checks.

> **The Golden Rule:** Spend 70% of your time on Planning. 30% on Creation and Validation combined. This ratio is what separates good videos from forgettable ones.

### P: Planning (Spend 70% of your time here)

Planning is the most critical step. Most videos fail because teams jump to recording too fast. Start with the persona, not the product.

- Fill out a video brief before writing a single word of script
- Write a script structured around Tell-Show-Tell (Context, Capability, Impact)
- Storyboard every screen you plan to show
- Run through the flow dry at least twice to check logic and timing

### C: Creation (Tools, Recording, and Editing)

Execute the plan. Start with strict environmental control before hitting record.

- Prepare a safe demo sandbox. Never use live customer data
- Close all unnecessary applications and turn off notifications
- Camera at eye level, lit from the front, external microphone for audio
- Record using the right tool: Synthesia for AI avatar videos, Descript for screen and human-cam
- Edit in Descript: cut the transcript, remove filler words with AI, trim silences, add B-roll

### V: Validation (The Final Quality Check)

Validation ensures your video has both technical integrity and persona resonance. Do not skip this step.

- Run the 13-point checklist (persona hook within 5 seconds, demo share 60%+, spoken CTA matches final slide)
- Use AI quality audits: paste your script into Claude to check Tell-Show-Tell structure, active voice, and benefit statements
- Technical check: realistic data, clean UI, steady cursor movements, no customer PII visible

## Video Levels: From Teaser to Deep Dive {#video-levels}

Use different formats for different stages of the buying journey.

| Level | Type | Length | Purpose | Funnel Stage |
|-------|------|--------|---------|--------------|
| L1 | Teaser / Overview | 60-90 sec | Spark interest. One wow moment. | Top of Funnel |
| L2 | Product Demo (On-Demand) | 3-5 min | Show one use case per persona. | Mid Funnel |
| L3 | Deep Dive Demo | 5-10 min (max) | End-to-end workflow for one process. | Mid Funnel |
| L4 | Industry Use Case | 5-10 min | Full E2E supply chain story. | Evaluation |
| L5 | Live Demo / PoC Recording | 20-45 min | Prospect-specific. Record for reuse. | Bottom of Funnel |

> **Keep It Short:** Teaser demos: 2-3 minutes max. Deep dives: small chunks of 5 minutes, never more than 10 minutes for complex topics. If your deep dive runs longer, split it into multiple videos.

### Level 1: Teaser / Overview (60-90 sec)

Your movie trailer. Show one powerful moment that makes the viewer want more. No full workflow. No slides. Just the single best screen your product offers.

**Timing Formula:**
- TELL 1 (15 sec): One sharp question about the persona's pain
- SHOW (55 sec): The single most impressive product screen
- TELL 2 (20 sec): Value statement + one CTA

### Level 2: Product Demo On-Demand (3-5 min)

Your workhorse video. One persona. One use case. One process. These videos go into goConsensus to build bigger stories.

**Timing Formula:**
- TELL 1 (60 sec): Persona scenario. One challenge slide. Who watches this and what they will see
- SHOW (3 min): Fullscreen demo. Persona user flow, not a feature tour
- TELL 2 (30-60 sec): Three outcomes achieved. Value statement. Single CTA

### Level 3: Deep Dive Demo (5-10 min)

Goes deeper into one workflow. Use it for qualified leads who want to understand the system end-to-end. Keep it under 10 minutes. If you need more, split into separate videos.

**Timing Formula:**
- TELL 1 (90 sec): Technical context. Persona, use case, system environment. Max 2 slides
- SHOW (7-8 min): End-to-end workflow demo. Handle 1-2 objections mid-demo
- TELL 2 (90 sec): ROI, time saved, risk reduced. CTA for follow-up

### Level 4: Industry Use Case Demo (5-10 min)

These tell the full End-to-End supply chain story for one industry. The viewer first understands where they sit in the overall supply chain and what their role looks like. Then you connect the dots.

**Key Concept: E2E Supply Chain Position**

In supply chain, the viewer needs to understand two things first:
1. Where am I in the End-to-End supply chain process?
2. What is my persona or role in this process?

Once that is clear, show the specific use case. This context is what makes industry demos different from regular product demos.

### Level 5: Live Demo / PoC Recording (20-45 min)

Every live demo or proof of concept you deliver should be recorded. These recordings become your internal best practice library for coaching and training.

## The Core Framework: Tell-Show-Tell with Personas {#tell-show-tell}

Every video, at every level, follows the same structure. This is not optional. It is the backbone of every recording.

### Tell-Show-Tell Explained

**TELL 1 (Set the Stage):** Introduce the persona. Describe their challenge. Use 1-2 slides max. Connect with the viewer emotionally.

**SHOW (Demo in Action):** Fullscreen demo. No slides. Show the product solving the problem. Move the mouse slowly. Narrate what the persona achieves, not what buttons they click.

**TELL 2 (Value + CTA):** Summarize what you showed. State the value in concrete terms (time saved, errors reduced). End with one single call to action.

### The Slide Rule

Demo recordings should be demo-heavy, not slide-heavy.

- Minimum 60% of the video is live product demo (fullscreen)
- As few words as possible
- Max 1-2 slides for TELL 1 (setting the scene)
- Max 1 slide for TELL 2 (closing with value)
- No company overview slides. No feature list slides. No agenda slides for short videos

**Slide design principles:**
- Use big visuals and as little text as possible
- Make the supply chain process look simple and easy
- Use personas to cut down stories into small, digestible chunks

### Persona-Based Approach

Every video starts with a persona, not a product. Ask yourself:

- Who is watching this? (role, industry, seniority)
- What is their biggest challenge today?
- What does success look like for them?

## Planning Your Video (Most Important Step) {#planning}

Spend 70% of your time on planning and 30% on recording and editing.

### Step 1: Fill in the Video Brief

Before you write a single word, answer these questions:

| Field | Your Answer |
|-------|-------------|
| Video Type | Teaser / Product Demo / Deep Dive / Industry Use Case / Live Demo |
| Target Persona | Role, seniority, industry, company size |
| Top Pain Point | What keeps them up at night? |
| Industry Focus | Automotive, Pharma, Retail, Chemicals, and more |
| Product Focus | Max 1-2 modules or use cases per video |
| Hook (first 10 sec) | One question or statement that hits right away |
| Max Length | 60-90 sec / 3-5 min / 5-10 min |
| Format | Avatar (Synthesia) / Human Cam / Screen+Voice / Mix |
| Slides | Max 3-4 for videos under 5 min |
| Demo Share | No demo / Short clip / Fullscreen demo |
| Call-to-Action | What should the viewer do next? |
| Key Messages | Max 3 messages. If you need more, make two videos |

### Step 2: Write the Script

Use the timing formula for your video level. Write the script in a 4-column table: Time, Visual/Action, Narration, and Direction Notes. Your narration should sound like you are talking to a colleague. Keep sentences short. Use active voice. Address the viewer as "you".

### Step 3: Prepare the Demo Environment

- Use a sandbox or demo tenant. Never use live production data
- Run through the full flow twice before recording
- Close every application you do not need. Turn off notifications
- Set screen resolution to 1920x1080. Enable cursor highlight

### Step 4: Storyboard and Dry Run

Map out every screen you will show. For each screen, write down what the viewer sees, what you say, and how long it takes. Record a practice version. Watch it. Fix issues. Then record the real version.

## AI Prompts for the Full Video Lifecycle {#ai-prompts}

Use these prompts with Claude (or similar AI) to speed up every phase. Always use the RIGS method: **R**ole (who the AI acts as), **I**nstruction (what to do), **G**uardrails (format, tone, constraints), and **S**pecifics (context, audience, pain points).

Claude WT Project: https://claude.ai/project/019daa6e-a320-7021-b6df-196018748d98

> **Pro Tip:** Always fill in the bracketed specifics [like this]. AI knows nothing about your product, competitors, or buyer until you provide that context. The more specific you are, the better the output.

### Prompt 1: The Strategy Prompt (Generate a Video Brief)

Use this before writing a script. It forces the AI to define the strategic core of the video so it is tailored to your exact buyer.

```
You are a Senior PreSales Consultant specializing in Supply Chain & Global Trade SaaS.
I want to create a video. Help me generate a structured Video Brief.

My inputs:
- Video Type: [90-Second Overview / Product Demo / Deep Dive / Industry Use Case]
- Target Persona: [e.g. VP Supply Chain, Trade Compliance Manager]
- Industry: [e.g. Automotive, Pharma, Retail]
- Primary Business Problem: [e.g. export controls, customs processing]
- Desired Length: [max 90 sec / 3-5 min / 5-10 min]
- Call-to-Action: [request demo / book meeting / explore more]

Generate a complete brief with: Hook, Tell-Show-Tell structure, Key Messages (max 3),
one objection to address, and a value statement.
Keep language simple. Use active voice. Address the viewer as "you".
```

---

### Prompt 2: The ICP Pain Excavator

Use this to uncover the emotional pain points your persona faces every day. This gives you the raw material for powerful hooks.

```
You are a B2B buyer psychology expert.
My product: [describe your product/solution in 1-2 sentences]
My Ideal Customer Profile: [role, industry, company size]

Identify the top 5 daily emotional pain points this person experiences.
Write each as an internal monologue (what they think but do not say out loud).
For each pain point, suggest a 1-sentence video hook that hits it directly.
Keep it real and specific. No corporate language.
```

---

### Prompt 3: The 8-Second Hook Generator

The first 8 seconds decide if someone watches or scrolls. Use this to generate hooks that stop the scroll.

```
Write 5 different opening lines (under 20 words each) for a video targeting [PERSONA] about [TOPIC].

Use these formats:
1. A contrarian statement
2. A surprising statistic
3. A direct pain call-out
4. A bold prediction
5. A "what if" question

Rate each hook from 1-10 for "scroll-stop power" and explain why.
```

---

### Prompt 4: The Brand Voice Trainer

Feed the AI examples of your best content so it learns your tone. Then use that tone for every script going forward.

```
I am going to give you 3 examples of my best-performing written content.

[PASTE EXAMPLE 1]
[PASTE EXAMPLE 2]
[PASTE EXAMPLE 3]

Based on these, describe my brand voice in 5 bullet points
(tone, sentence length, vocabulary, structure, personality).

Then rewrite the following draft script using that exact voice:
[PASTE DRAFT SCRIPT]
```

---

### Prompt 5: The Full Script Assembler

Once you have a brief, use this to generate a production-ready script.

```
Use the following Video Brief and generate a complete video script.

[PASTE YOUR VIDEO BRIEF HERE]

Format the script as a table with 4 columns:
- Column 1: TIME (e.g. 0:00-0:30)
- Column 2: VISUAL / ACTION (what is visible on screen)
- Column 3: NARRATION (spoken text, natural and conversational)
- Column 4: DIRECTION NOTE (tone, pace, emphasis)

Rules:
- Follow Tell-Show-Tell strictly
- Demo share minimum 60% for demo-type videos
- No feature listing. Frame every benefit from the persona's view
- Keep sentences short. Use active voice. Use "you" language
- Do not start sentences with "We". Start with "You" or the outcome
- Include only one clear CTA at the end
```

---

### Prompt 6: The Conversion Optimizer (Self-Audit)

Use this to catch conversion killers before you record. The AI acts as a ruthless direct-response copywriter.

```
You are a senior direct-response copywriter who has optimized hundreds of video scripts.

Audit the following script:
[PASTE SCRIPT HERE]

Rewrite any sentences that:
- Start with "We" (flip to viewer perspective)
- List a feature without a business benefit
- Exceed 30 spoken words for a 10-second window

Give an overall conversion score out of 10 and explain your rating.
```

---

### Prompt 7: The Traffic-Light Quality Review

Your final quality gate before recording. Paste your script and get a clear pass/fail per criterion.

```
Review the following video script against these criteria:
[PASTE SCRIPT HERE]

Check structure:
1. Is the persona addressed within the first 15 seconds?
2. Is Tell-Show-Tell clearly visible? Mark where SHOW begins and TELL 2 starts
3. Are there more than 3 feature mentions without a benefit statement? Flag them
4. Is the CTA clear and singular?
5. What is the demo share in %? (Target: min 60% for demo videos)

Check language:
6. Are there technical terms the target persona would not understand?
7. Is the voice active, not passive?
8. Does the narration sound conversational or like a white paper?
9. Are there vague phrases like "many features" or "various capabilities"?

Output: Traffic-light rating (GREEN / AMBER / RED) per criterion + concrete improvement suggestions.
```

### Prompt 8: The Prospect Customization Prompt

Use this when you need to tailor an existing demo flow for a specific buyer or industry.

```
You are a sales engineer and demo specialist. I am preparing a product demonstration
for [PROSPECT TYPE/ROLE] in [INDUSTRY] with specific use cases around [KEY REQUIREMENTS].

Prompt me for any missing variables before responding.

Create a demo customization framework that:
- Aligns product features to the prospect's documented needs
- Uses their actual data/scenarios when possible
- Demonstrates ROI through their specific metrics
- Handles technical questions and limitations transparently

Include: Pre-demo discovery questions, a demo flow with timing (30/45/60-minute versions),
interactive elements to increase engagement, and competitor differentiation talking points.
```

Use data from published videos to train the AI to write better scripts over time.

```

Your job is to analyze what worked, what did not, and rewrite the underperforming sections.

Video 1: [TITLE]
- Average view duration: [X seconds]
- Biggest drop-off point: [X seconds]
- Top comment theme: [X]

Video 2: [TITLE] - [Add stats]
Video 3: [TITLE] - [Add stats]

Based on this data:
1. Identify 3 patterns causing drop-off
2. Suggest structural changes for my next script
3. Rewrite the underperforming section from Video 1 using what you learned
4. Give me a retention-optimization checklist I can apply to every script going forward
```

## AI-Powered Editing Prompts (Descript) {#descript-prompts}

Descript has built-in AI features ("Underlord") that let you edit with conversational prompts.

**Edit for Clarity**
Use the "Edit for clarity" action to have AI analyze your transcript and cut unnecessary tangents, dead air, and long-winded sentences automatically.

**Remove Filler Words**
Use "Remove filler words" to instantly delete "um", "uh", "you know", "basically", and "like" from both the transcript and the video timeline.

**Shorten Word Gaps**
Set the gap remover to max 0.3 second pauses. This tightens pacing without making it feel rushed.

**Add Background Music**
Type conversational commands like "Can you add some music that fits the vibe?" If the result is too loud, follow up with "Decrease the volume."

**Create Clips for Social**
Instead of scrubbing through a long video manually, prompt the AI to "Create clips" or "Create a highlight reel". It scans the transcript for engaging moments and cuts them into short vertical videos for LinkedIn, TikTok, or Reels. It even provides a virality score for each clip.

## Recording Tools and Best Practices {#recording-tools}

### Home Office Setup

Choose a quiet, separate workspace with an ergonomic chair, proper desk, and stable high-speed internet. You need a laptop or PC, external webcam, microphone, and at least one good monitor.

Put the camera at eye level with a clean, real background behind you (no virtual background), some distance from the wall, and only a few non-distracting personal items.

Use one main soft key light about 3-5 ft away at 45 degrees, ideally 1,000-2,000 lumens at 5,500K. Add a softer fill and a small back light for a more polished look.

More information: [Homeoffice Setup - PreSales Handbook](https://www.presales-handbook.com/avada_portfolio/homeoffice-setup/)

### Tool Decision Matrix

| Video Level | TELL 1 Tool | SHOW Tool | TELL 2 Tool | Assembly |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| L1: Teaser | Synthesia | Screen (Descript) | Synthesia | goConsensus |
| L2: Product Demo | Synthesia or Cam | Screen (Descript) | Synthesia or Cam | goConsensus |
| L3: Deep Dive | Cam or Slides | Screen (Descript) | Cam or Slides | goConsensus |
| L4: Industry Use Case | Synthesia + Slides | Screen (Descript) | Synthesia + Slides | goConsensus |
| L5: Live Demo | Live (Teams/Zoom) | Live Screen | Live | Internal Library |

### Demo Recording Golden Rules

- Always fullscreen. No split screen. No distractions
- Use your sandbox or demo tenant. Never live production data
- Show a real user flow from the persona's perspective. Not admin functions
- Move the mouse slowly. Viewers cannot follow fast movements
- Enable cursor highlight for important clicks
- Run through the flow dry at least twice before recording
- No feature tours. Do not say "And over here you can also see..."

### Audio, Camera, and Environment

- Use an external microphone. Your laptop mic is not good enough
- Record in a quiet room with the door closed. Use noise cancellation if needed
- Position camera at eye level. Light from the front, not behind
- Avoid virtual backgrounds if possible. Use a real, clean background
- Dress professionally. Avoid stripes, neon colors, and shiny accessories

## Cutting and Editing {#editing}

### Editing Workflow in Descript

1. Import all clips (avatar, screen, camera) into one Descript project
2. Script-based editing: delete text = delete video
3. Remove filler words using the AI action
4. Trim silences: set gap remover to max 0.3 second pauses
5. Add B-roll: overlay screen clips on top of presenter audio
6. Use simple cuts for transitions. No fancy effects
7. Add captions/subtitles (essential for LinkedIn autoplay on mute)

### Export Settings

- Format: MP4 (H.264), minimum 1080p, 16:9 aspect ratio
- For LinkedIn feed: consider 1:1 aspect ratio
- Create a custom thumbnail (persona image + headline text)
- Export SRT captions from Descript and upload separately

## End-to-End Workflow: Idea to Publish {#e2e-workflow}

## Quick Reference Card {#quick-reference}

### 5 Golden Rules

1. Persona FIRST, not product
2. Hook within the first 10 seconds
3. Tell-Show-Tell ALWAYS
4. Demo fullscreen, min 60% (for demo types)
5. ONE CTA, not three

### 5 Most Common Mistakes

1. Long intro jingle or logo animation
2. Slide wall instead of demo
3. Feature tour without business benefit
4. Multiple CTAs at the end
5. Wrong video type for funnel stage
