## Vector 1 — Tariff actions by trade lane / HS chapter — **Score: 5**

**Evidence chain:**

  risk factors, p. 14. China share of production "approximately 60%" 🟢 Confirmed — CFO,
  Q1 FY2026 earnings call, in response to an analyst tariff question.
- Product set (wireless audio, smart-home hubs, accessories) falls in consumer-electronics
  HS chapters covered by the active additional-tariff actions on China-origin goods
  🟡 Inferred — product-category-to-chapter mapping is ours; unambiguous for headphones
  and hubs, done without customs records.
- Quantified impact: "incremental tariff costs reduced gross margin by approximately 180
  basis points in fiscal 2025" 🟢 Confirmed — 10-K MD&A, p. 41.
- Responses already forced: announced retail price increases on two product lines
  🟢 Confirmed — Q1 FY2026 call, CEO prepared remarks; Vietnam capacity expansion "to
  roughly half of unit volume by fiscal 2027" 🟢 Confirmed — same call. Note the target is
  fiscal 2027 — today the majority of value still moves on the China lane 🟡 Inferred —
  arithmetic from the 60% figure above.
- Exclusion status: no product exclusions identified for their chapters on the current
  exclusion lists 🟡 Inferred — list check 2026-07-01; company-specific filings not searchable
  without entry data.

**Score justification (against the anchors):** Matches the 5-anchor on all three clauses:
majority of import value in lanes/chapters under active actions ("approximately 60%" China
production into covered electronics chapters, with further escalation proposed on those
chapters as of the June docket 🟢 Confirmed — regulator docket, checked 2026-07-01); filings
quantify a tariff hit to gross margin (180 bps); visible forced responses (price increases,
disclosed supplier-base shift). This is not a 3: mitigation is underway but the quantified
hit and majority-lane concentration are present-tense, not prospective.

**Open hardening questions:** import value by lane (needs customs records or the customer);
Vietnam-shift actual progress vs the fiscal-2027 target; whether any exclusion requests are

## So-what — where the urgency is (capability level)

- **Tariff exposure (V1, scored 5) is a live P&L wound, not a risk** — 180 bps of margin
  already gone, majority-lane concentration still in place through at least fiscal 2027.
  Anything that changes duty outcomes now — correct classification at chapter level,
  origin management as volume shifts to Vietnam, duty-recovery mechanisms on what's already
  been paid — has a quantified baseline to be measured against. This is the opening conversation.
- **Forced-labor exposure (V4, scored 4) is a continuity risk with a documentation answer** —
  their gap is not intent, it's traceability depth: nothing published beyond tier 1 while
  their most detention-prone input sits in an active product line. Capability conversation:
  supply-chain mapping below tier 1 and detention-response documentation readiness —
  measured in days-to-release when goods stop at the border.
- **De-minimis exposure (V5, scored 4) lands on their growth engine** — the channel they
  told investors is fastest-growing is the one whose entry regime is being legislated away,
  and nothing disclosed says they've modeled it. Capability conversation: landed-cost
  simulation of the proposed regime against their real order profile, before they commit to
  EU fulfillment architecture.
- **Vector 2's blank is itself an agenda item** — "we could not screen your supply base from
  public data" is an honest, credible first-meeting line that invites them to open the
  counterparty list, which also unblocks half the gap list above.
- V3 (scored 2) is a monitoring item, not an urgency lever — raise it only as evidence of
  thoroughness, with the scope-extension review as the tripwire.
