# Extreme Ownership
**by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin**

> If something goes wrong — even if it is not directly your fault — you still own the outcome. Take complete ownership, not only of what you are responsible for but also of everything that affects your mission. Make no excuses. Instead of complaining about challenges, focus on developing solutions and resolving problems.

---

## No Bad Teams, Only Bad Leaders

The leader drives performance — or doesn't.

It is not what you preach, it is what you tolerate.

---

## Believe in the Mission

If you don't truly believe in what you are doing, your team won't either.

The leader must explain not just what to do, but why.

Ask questions to understand and believe in the mission.

---

## Check the Ego

Ego can cloud judgment and cause disruptions.

It is not about you. It is about the mission and finding the best way to accomplish it.

---

## Discipline and Focus

A good leader should be confident, but not arrogant. Brave, but not reckless.

A leader must know when to lead and when to follow.

Be humble, not passive. Take full responsibility for your actions while also allowing others to take charge when needed.

---

## Discipline Equals Freedom

Keeping things simple is key to success.

Plans and orders should be shared clearly and directly.

When problems happen, added complexity makes things worse.

---

## Prioritise and Execute

Take a moment to relax, look around, and make a call.

Stay calm and make the best decisions possible.

Tackle the most important problem first. After you solve that, move on to the next one. Do not try to do everything at once.

Focusing on one goal can help bring the whole team together.

**Clarity before speed.** Do not rush to look decisive. Make sure everyone understands the goal before you move.

---

## Cover and Move

People and teams need to work together, communicate clearly, and support each other.

Eliminate the "us versus them" mindset.

---

## Decentralised Command

Every leader at every level must be empowered and trusted to lead within the mission.

Decentralised command does not mean people do their own thing. Without alignment, that results in chaos.

**Default to trust (but verify).** Assume your people want to succeed. Give them space to act, but check results and offer feedback.

---

## Leading Up and Down

Talk clearly to your team and your leaders. Explain what you need and why.

Do not assume others know what you mean.

Leadership goes both ways. It flows down from leaders and also comes up from team members.

One key task of any leader is to support their own boss.

**Calm is contagious.** Your tone sets the culture.

---

## Execution and Performance

### Plan

Good planning includes clear objectives, roles, and contingency thinking.

Everyone must understand not just what to do, but why.

A plan should include: overall purpose, commander's intent, risks to mitigate, contingencies, and specific courses of action.

**Mission Clarity Framework:**
- What is the mission?
- Why is it important?
- Who is responsible for what?
- What does success look like?
- What are the key risks and contingencies?

### Default: Aggressive, Not Reckless

Take action. Do not wait for perfect information or ideal timing. Move fast but smart.

### Prioritise and Execute

Tackle the most critical problem first. Then move to the next one.

### Always Debrief and Learn

After every project, meeting, or failure — stop and ask: what did we learn?

**Brief — Execute — Debrief — Adjust.**

### Uncertainty

The picture is never clear. The outcome is never certain.

Leaders must be comfortable amid pressure and chaos, and act on logic, not emotion.

Leaders must make decisions promptly, then be ready to adjust quickly.

### Two Is One, One Is None

Redundancy matters. Have backup owners, a second option, a spare vendor.

---

## Growth and Balance

### Training Is a Leader's Job

Leaders are directly responsible for making sure their teams are trained, capable, and confident.

If the team fails, it is not a training department issue. It is a leadership failure.

### Repetition Builds Discipline

Repetition turns knowledge into instinct.

Disciplined, consistent training gives freedom in real situations.

### Humility and Continuous Learning

Even when you lead, you are still a student.

Learn from mistakes, from your team, and from competitors.

### Detachment: Step Back to See Clearly

When emotions rise, take a mental step back.

Look at the situation from above before reacting.

### No Victim Mindset

No excuses. No "because of them" or "the system."

Always look for what you can influence.

### Teach Ownership

Help others take responsibility and grow.

---

## Slow Is Smooth, Smooth Is Fast

**Slow is smooth** means take your time and do it right. Be deliberate and focused rather than rushing. When you go too fast, you make mistakes, forget steps, and lose control.

**Smooth is fast** means once you have practised and mastered a task so it becomes smooth and precise, you will naturally become faster — without forcing speed.

---

## Keep It Simple

**Brief — Execute — Debrief — Adjust.**

---

## Leadership Is Simple, But Not Easy

A good leader has nothing to prove, yet everything to prove.

With Extreme Ownership, any person can develop into a highly effective leader.

**Lead and win.**

---

## Key Phrases

- "I own that."
- "What can I do to help us fix it?"
- "Let's simplify this."
- "What is our priority right now?"
- "Do you clearly understand why we are doing this?"
