# 1000 Years PreSales — Interview Insights
**by Dr. Johannes Hangl**

15 interviews with experienced PreSales professionals from across the world.
Combined experience: over 1,000 years in PreSales.

---

## The Core Questions Asked in Every Interview

1. What attracted you to PreSales, and how has your journey shaped your approach?
2. What key skills should every PreSales professional develop, and how do you continuously improve them?
3. What bad habits might PreSales professionals have that could hinder their progress?
4. What advice would you give to someone transitioning into PreSales?
5. How do you create engaging and effective demos?
6. How do you stay current with industry trends and technological advancements?
7. How do you balance deep technical expertise with strong communication skills?
8. How do you handle difficult clients or objections?
9. How do you foster collaboration between PreSales and Sales?
10. How do you coach and develop junior PreSales professionals?
11. How do you maintain a healthy work-life balance?

## Interview 1 — Brendan Lynch
**Solution Consultant, Fink (Contract Lifecycle Management), Vienna**

### Journey into PreSales
Brendan studied computer science at a liberal arts college in the US. After his first internship, he realised he disliked writing code alone all day. He liked the bigger picture — understanding problems and what a solution might look like. He identified two paths: product manager or technical person who talks to customers. He chose PreSales.

### Role of a PreSales Consultant
"If I had to put it in a couple of words, the PreSales consultant's role is to communicate solutions and communicate value at a lot of different levels."

PreSales communicates value from the first cold engagement all the way through technical discovery, champion-building, and final decision-maker conversations. Everything is communication.

### Key Skills
1. **Communication, communication, communication.** Numbers one, two, and three. Technical skills matter, but the ability to communicate value is what separates top performers.
2. **Problem solving.** Learn quickly and apply knowledge across different contexts.
3. **Continuous learning.** Things change constantly. If you can't adapt, you stall.

### Bad Habits
The most common pitfall: **getting too deep into technical details too quickly.**

"We tend to find the technical stuff super interesting. But what we're interested in is not what the customer cares about at all."

### Advice for Someone New
"It's about the money. It's always about the money. Everything you do, whoever you're interacting with — ask yourself: what is going to help them make more money? That's why customers buy things."

## Interview 2 — Zsuzsi Szentesi
**Sales Engineering Manager (European Team), London**

### Journey into PreSales
Zsuzsi spent 15 years in sales before moving to Sales Engineering. She realised she had more passion for technical consultancy and finding real solutions for customers than for pure selling. She progressed from SE to Senior SE to Team Lead to managing the European team.

### Why PreSales over Sales
"As a Sales Engineer, you are obliged to say the truth. You actually have to make sure the solution will fit the customer and will work. It's your job to make sure that what you say is actually true."

PreSales is more consultative and relationship-driven. Less pressure than an AE role, but still impactful.

### Key Skills
- Technical consultancy combined with genuine customer empathy.
- Honest communication — you are the voice of truth in the deal.
- Continuous learning and staying current with technology.

## Interview 3 — Faraz Hussain
**PreSales Professional, 10 years experience across petrochemicals, IoT, and SaaS**

### Journey into PreSales
Faraz arrived in PreSales by accident — he applied as a business analyst, was put in an account management role, and then given the title of Sales Engineer. He has since worked in petrochemicals, IoT, and marketing SaaS.

### Key Skills
1. **Adaptability.** No two customer scenarios are the same. Be ready to adapt without going off script.
2. **Active listening.** Customers tell PreSales things they won't tell the AE. Be the trusted consultant.
3. **Always be learning.** "Know everything about something and something about everything."

### On Being the Customer's Champion
"PreSales are the customer's champions. Customers will sometimes pull you aside and mention things they haven't told the AE. That's when the consultant role comes in — I hear your problem, I see your struggle. Let me see what I can do for you."

## Interview 5 — Hena Kless
**Senior Solution Consultant, LastPass**

### On What PreSales Is
"PreSales is the best job you've never heard of."

"There's a really nice place we fill — between a customer and the technology, between a business case and the technology. That's where we fit in really well."

### Key Skills
1. **Curiosity** — not just a skill, but a mindset. Always wanting to dig deeper.
2. **Continuous learning** — "Never think you know everything. Always be aware of what you don't know."
3. **Training like an athlete** — Talk to your team, practice with your AEs, always ask for feedback.

### Balancing Technical and Communication
"Imagine being at a family gathering and a relative asks you to fix their printer or wifi. How you help them — translating complex tech into a simple answer they can use — is exactly what PreSales does."

"The only thing that matters to them is: how do I get internet? How do I print something? Connect their technical problem with easy-to-understand value."

## Interview 6 — Jonathan Carvalho
**Solution Engineer, Cloudflare**

### Journey into PreSales
Jonathan wanted to combine his engineering background with soft skills — communication, influencing, and negotiation. PreSales was the perfect fit.

### Balancing Technical and Communication
Adapt to the client type:
- **Enterprise clients** — More stakeholders, longer timelines. Your communication skills matter most. Bring in specialists for deep technical topics.
- **SMB clients** — Less internal resource. You may need to go deeper technically to help them more directly.

"You have to adapt yourself as a Solution Engineer to provide what the customer actually needs."

### Staying Current
Schedule dedicated time in your calendar — one to two hours per week — to review new features, read industry news, and learn what is changing.

## Interview 9 — Toby Allen
**Solutions Engineer, Okta (Identity Management), 15 years in PreSales**

### On Balancing Technical Depth and Communication
"The core skill for a PreSales professional is the ability to learn a substantial part of a product and grasp core concepts of how the technology works — not to solve every single problem end to end."

"Your job is to identify the technology that will solve the problem and communicate how that will happen to both business and technical stakeholders."

Today, customers often know more about your product than you think. Your value is in translating your experience from other businesses and industries to their specific situation.

### On Discovery
"A very key skill is the ability to do discovery — to uncover the real technical problem. A customer will often ask 'can your product do X?' but they're asking because they're trying to solve a business problem. Your job is to find that underlying problem."

## Interview 11 — Steve Bullington
**Early Talent Development, Salesforce (Solutions Engineers), 30 years in technology**

### Journey into PreSales
Steve spent 30 years in sales and sales leadership across telecommunications. He eventually moved into early talent development, building a Sales University for account executives. He joined Salesforce to build and run their early talent development programme for Solutions Engineers — 14 cohorts and 265 associates later.

### Key Skills
He believes in the principle from James Clear's Atomic Habits: **get 1% better every day.**

"As a technology professional — not just PreSales — we have to focus on getting better every single day. If you look back three years ago, none of us were talking about large language models. The landscape changes constantly. The people who win are the ones who commit to continuous improvement."

### On Developing Junior PreSales Professionals
Build structured learning programmes. Create intentional practice opportunities. Feedback loops are essential — people need to know where they are strong and where they need to grow.

## Interview 13 — Lukas
**PreSales Professional**

### On Collaboration Between PreSales and Sales
Build a strong relationship with your AE. Align before every customer meeting. Debrief after every call. Share information openly — what the customer said, what they care about, what concerns came up.

The moment PreSales and Sales operate as separate silos, the customer feels it. They notice when the two teams are not aligned.

## Interview 14 — Natasja Bax
**PreSales Professional and Thought Leader**

### On Demo Best Practices
- Start with the customer's world, not your product.
- Build the story around their pain, not your features.
- Use personas. Speak as the user. "I am Kevin, I do this every day, and here is my problem."
- Ask questions throughout. A monologue is not a demo.

"The best demos feel like a conversation, not a presentation."

### On Knowledge and Learning
Stay connected to the PreSales community — PreSales Collective, LinkedIn groups, and industry events. Learn from peers as much as from formal training.

## Interview 15 — Katharina Baum
**PreSales Professional**

### On Staying Current
Use AI tools to summarise industry trends, release notes, and news. Prompt AI with specific questions like: "What are the top five things Sales Engineers are doing differently in 2025?"

Stay connected to thought leaders like Peter Cohan (Great Demo!). Read, apply, and share what you learn with your team.

### On Work-Life Balance
Set clear boundaries. Protect your focus time. PreSales is intense — you carry the demo, the discovery, the PoC, and the objections all at once. If you do not actively protect your time, it disappears.

## Recurring Themes Across All 15 Interviews

### The Top Skills of a Great PreSales Professional

1. **Communication** — Mentioned by almost every interviewee as the number-one skill. Not just talking, but listening, translating technical concepts, and adapting to the audience.

2. **Curiosity** — A mindset more than a skill. Always asking why, always wanting to understand more. Multiple interviewees cited this as the trait they look for when hiring.

3. **Continuous learning** — Technology changes. Markets change. The professionals who stay on top keep learning every week, not just when they feel behind.

4. **Adaptability** — No two customers are the same. Being able to read a situation and adjust your approach is essential.

5. **Active listening** — Customers tell PreSales things they won't tell Sales. You create that trust by listening more than you talk.

6. **Domain expertise** — The best PreSales professionals know their industry deeply, not just their product. This is what makes you a trusted advisor.

### The Most Common Bad Habits

- **Going too deep into technical detail too quickly.** Customers care about outcomes, not features.
- **Not listening enough.** Talking too much and not asking enough questions.
- **Skipping discovery.** Running demos before understanding the real problem.
- **Focusing on what interests you, not what the customer needs.**
- **Not documenting.** Losing insights that could help the rest of the deal team.

### Advice for Someone New to PreSales

- Lead with curiosity. Ask more than you tell.
- Focus on business outcomes. It is always about the customer's money, time, or risk.
- Build your communication skills deliberately. Practice every demo, debrief every call.
- Get comfortable with ambiguity. PreSales is rarely linear.
- Find a mentor inside your organisation. Learn from someone who has done it well.
- Document everything. The OSD is your most important artefact.
