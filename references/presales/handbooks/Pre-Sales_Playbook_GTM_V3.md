## Core Value Formula

**Prove Your Value > All Others** (do nothing, competitors, other projects)

```
Value = Potential Value * Probability of Project Success
      = (Benefits - Costs) * Risk
```

### Stage Entry and Exit Criteria

**Stage 20 - Qualify**
- Entry: Customer has expressed interest in [COMPANY] solutions; discuss indicative pricing to determine budget fit; for RFx: qualify fit/strategy

## Long-Term Development (LTD)

**Objective:** Capture potential future opportunities and build a plan to move them into the sales pipeline. Focus on a multi-year roadmap for opportunities not expected to close within 12 months.

### Responsibilities

- **Sales Development:** Maintain relationships with potential clients and follow up regularly.
- **Sales:** Maintain relationships with potential clients and follow up regularly.
- **PreSales:** Support sales with market research and insights.
- **Professional Services:** Stay informed of industry trends and customer challenges.
- **Product Management:** Use market insights to inform product development.

### Qualification Criteria

- Prospect shows interest in your solution but has no clear opportunity.
- Opportunity will not close within the next 12 months.

### Do's and Don'ts

- Do: Keep communication channels open and stay informed on prospect's business developments.
- Do: Share relevant updates.
- Do: Add to the marketing newsletter.
- Don't: Push prospects into the sales process prematurely.
- Don't: Neglect to update your CRM with LTD information.

**Tools:** Salesforce for CRM management.

## Stage 10: Sales Accepted Lead

### Goal
Get a meeting. Determine if the lead has potential and should be pursued.

### Key Responsibilities

**SDR:**
- Review the BANT form and ask questions (for SDR-sourced leads).

**Sales:**
- For SDR-sourced leads: review the BANT form from SDR.
- For ASD-sourced leads: complete a fully detailed BANT form and attach to the opportunity.
- Contact the prospect to schedule a qualification call.
- If disqualifying out: share insights with SDR and complete the disqualification reason in Salesforce.

### Do's and Don'ts

- Do: Maintain a pipeline with five times your quota.
- Do: Ask partners (e.g., Accenture) for referrals.
- Do: Respond to leads promptly and determine lead potential.
- Do: Reach out quickly after the lead is provided.
- Do: Read the BANT form attached to the opportunity.
- Do: Discuss with the SDR any questions about the BANT form.
- Do: Use Salesforce for lead management and keep it up to date.
- Don't: Wait to follow up. They may also be contacting your competitors.

### Qualification Criteria

- Spoke with the target, agreed on next steps, and there is a reasonable expectation that the opportunity will close within 12 months.

### Disqualification Criteria

- Prospect doesn't have a need for the solution, lacks budget or authority, or is unresponsive.

### Moving to the Next Stage

**Qualify In:** Put into Stage 20 in Salesforce.

**Qualify Out:** Close/Lost Analysis with close type and reason for closing.

## Stage 10: Best Practice for Prospecting

To effectively prospect, maintain a healthy pipeline (minimum 5x your quota), engage early, and personalize your approach.

- Schedule consistent prospecting time daily.
- Be available when buyers need you, especially during the consideration stage.
- Initiate first contact early in the decision-making process.
- Leverage digital platforms and social media.
- Focus on the prospect's needs and preferences.
- Research your contacts and tailor outreach.
- Aim to connect with decision-makers like VPs or executives.
- Leverage existing relationships and work with partners.
- Craft a compelling, personalized value proposition.

## Stage 20: Qualify (Sales Discovery)

### Goal
Decide whether to participate. Assess the prospect's pain points, solution fit, and willingness to engage in BANT and value hypothesis discussions.

### Key Responsibilities

**SDR:**
- Share insights about the prospect with the sales team.
- Collaborate with Sales to ensure a smooth handoff.

**Sales:**
- Get qualification criteria (spend, inventory, current system, pain).
- Create SharePoint folder (first check if one exists).
- Execute sales discovery and keep notes.
- Check Salesforce for historical opportunities.
- Start the Mutual Evaluation Plan and set clear next steps.
- Uncover potential objections and obstacles.
- Execute BANT assessment.
- Create or update Paperflite; share content and videos.
- For RFx: qualify fit/strategy. If you decide to bid, create the RFx intake in Salesforce.
- Create SC Request in Salesforce if help is needed for qualification.

**Pre-Sales:**
- Qualification support (only if needed).
- For RFx: check the RFx document to understand whether to participate. Note: for a blind RFx, an OSD will not exist.

**Prof. Services:**
- For RFx: review the RFx and provide ROM estimates.

### Do's and Don'ts

- Do: Understand who to bring into the process early as executive sponsor. Aim High: VP or higher.
- Do: Have the customer prove they can support indicative pricing. Target market is > $1B revenue.
- Do: Provide an RFx template that aligns to [COMPANY]'s differentiation strengths. Customize the cover letter (mandatory).
- Don't: Sell products into industries without references to support your value story.
- Don't: Waste time on low-potential leads or pressure leads into moving forward before they're ready.
- Don't: If communication has slowed, assume competitors have already made inroads.
- Don't: Waste time with blind RFxs.

### Why Assess Ability to Win?

- Don't waste your precious time.
- "I'd rather be last than #2."
- Bad processes often end in no decision.
- Walking away is a tool to drive further engagement.
- Don't burn bridges.

### How to Spend Time on Winnable Deals

**Test the ROI:**
- Size matters. Many solutions require a company with > $1B revenue. Qualify to indicative pricing.
- If client is evaluating [COMPANY] against their incumbent ERP, forewarnn leadership.
- Do you have references for this product in this industry?

**Test the Evaluation Plan:**
- For upsell or cross-sell, a joint evaluation plan is critical.
- If the stakeholder evaluation team exceeds 5 individuals, be cautious. Teams beyond 5 tend to take twice as long to decide.
- Check if IT, security, architecture, finance, and procurement are involved.

**Qualify the RFP/RFI:**
- Did you know it was coming? Did you write it?
- Use RFx primer templates. Set traps and guide requirements to your functional and commercial advantages.
- Qualify out and make them come back if they don't select someone.

**RFP Do's and Don'ts:**

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Actively qualify whether to participate | Blindly respond |
| Ask for a meeting with the SVP Supply Chain | Allow procurement to limit access |
| Get an NDA in place on your paper | Cut and paste from someone else's RFP |
| Push back on short turnaround timetables. Get 10 business days | Route for review the day before it's due |
| Require discovery before submission | |

### Moving to the Next Stage

**Qualify In:**
- At least 3 of 4 BANT present.
- If there is an RFx, the RFx request/intake is created.
- BANT plan and issues discussed with coach.
- Mutual Evaluation Plan delivered.
- NDA presented.

**Qualify Out:** Close/Lost Analysis.

**Tools:** Salesforce, Indicative Pricing, OSD with Sales Discovery, Paperflite, IronClad for NDA.

## Stage 30: Discovery (Technical Discovery)

> **Never ever do a demo without discovery!**

### Goal
Win strategy and coach identification. Discover customer pain points, business objectives, and organizational structure to frame the value proposition.

### Key Responsibilities

**Sales:**
- Help update the OSD with the Situation Sheet (Critical Business Issues, Problems and Reasons, Capabilities, Delta, and Value Realization Event).
- Use effective questioning techniques (e.g., SPIN framework).
- Finalize Evaluation Plan and create the Close Plan.
- Create SC Request in Salesforce.
- Develop Org Chart (Champion, Decision-Maker, Coach, Distractor, etc.).
- Conduct discovery meetings with key stakeholders.
- Collaborate with PreSales and Product Management.
- Involve VP Sales early for executive relationship building.
- Solidify coach and enable them to sell internally.

**Pre-Sales:**
- Understand the customer's technical environment and requirements.
- Work with Sales to create product demonstrations.
- Present the opportunity to PS/PM for gaps, multi-app complexities, or complex needs (WOSR).
- Update the OSD with the Situation Sheet.
- Perform Look and Feel Demo and/or Standard Demo (only if appropriate).

**Prof. Services:**
- Create ROM estimate / ballpark pricing.
- Leverage discovery insights for detailed project planning.
- Collaborate with Sales and PreSales on customer requirements.
- Support Sales and PreSales in addressing customer concerns.

**Product:**
- Collaborate to ensure product fit.
- Use feedback from Sales and PreSales to refine product offerings.
- Provide product-specific insights.

### Do's and Don'ts

- Do: Ask open-ended questions, gather info on organizational structure, and confirm the evaluation plan.
- Do: Validate partner strategy and involvement.
- Don't: Skip discovery. The purpose of discovery is "figuring out how to win."
- Don't: Do demos without knowing the pain.

### How to Win in Discovery

**Figure Out How to Win:**
- Information is your most powerful negotiating tool. You get this during discovery.
- Align the customer to [COMPANY]'s differentiation.

**Solidify Coach and Decision Maker:**
- What are the sponsor's personal objectives (MBO)?
- Has the sponsor sponsored a cloud solution selection before?
- Do you have access to ultimate stakeholders in Finance and IT?
- Is funding existing OPEX? Then IT is likely your decision maker.
- Does your decision maker have relationships with SIs or connections to competitors?

**Use the Evaluation Plan:**
- 90% of projects submitted in CAPEX/OPEX meetings fail to get approved. Failure is usually a result of poor project presentation.
- Develop the evaluation plan with the customer.
- Ensure all stakeholders see the working evaluation plan.
- An evaluation plan is a mini-contract. When a company agrees to use one, it's a strong qualifier.
- Most important activity: before redlining any commercial document, an [COMPANY] executive meets with the signatory to re-validate execution expectations.

### Qualification Criteria

- Discovery meetings completed and next steps scheduled.
- Clear view on customer pain points and ROI.
- Org chart and power base chart developed. Decision-maker known.
- Evaluation plan confirmed verbally or in writing.

### Disqualification Criteria

- Target is unwilling to engage in discovery or provide necessary information.
- No relationship with management and no clear coach.
- No clear understanding of pain points, objectives, and decision-making process.

### Moving to the Next Stage

**Discovery Exit Criteria:**
- Decision Maker added as a contact.
- Discovery meetings complete.
- Evaluation Plan in progress.
- OSD in progress.
- Key next steps scheduled.
- NDA signed.
- Org and Power Base charts complete.
- Partner ecosystem known and partner assigned.

**Qualify Out Exit Activities:**
- Close/Lost Analysis.
- Post Deal Analysis Comments.
- Guidance to Product, Marketing, Business, and Competitive Intelligence.

### Technical Discovery - OSD
- Add Technical Discovery notes to the OSD.
- Use the template or add pages with your info.
- Make a summary in the main pages.
- If you need guidance: see Technical Discovery Guidebook.

### ROM (Rough Order of Magnitude) Pricing
1. Calculate the license fee via the Genus Quote Calculator.
2. Calculate the professional service effort using the PS ROM Estimator.
3. Use the Ballpark Pricing template (GTM Commercial Proposal Template V1_2023).

## Stage 40: Teach and Prove

### Goal
Prove your value is best. Convince the customer the solution can address their pain points and provide superior capabilities.

### Key Responsibilities

**Sales:**
- Have a conversation with the decision-maker before issuing a proposal.
- Update Paperflite and add recording.
- Know the names and titles of attendees and research their interests.
- Agree on a clear agenda before the presentation.
- Arrange product demos and presentations.
- Call and email attendees individually after the presentation.
- Handle objections and answer questions.
- Include contracts in your proposal.

**Pre-Sales:**
- Product Demonstrations: Create tailored demos that showcase value and address specific pain points.
- Technical Expertise: Answer questions about functionality, features, and compatibility.
- Service Discovery Support: Brief PS before they do the Service Discovery / Implementation Scoping.
- Documentation and Support: Provide technical documentation and support materials.
- Competitive Analysis: Prepare to address questions about how your product compares to competitors.
- Use the Best Practice Demo guidelines.
- Present the Opportunity to PS/PM in case of gaps (WOSR).

**Prof. Services:**
- Execute Service Discovery / Implementation Scoping to create SOW once a technical win is achieved.
- Collaborate with Sales and PreSales on tailored solutions.

**Product:**
- Collaborate with Product Management to ensure the solution aligns with customer needs.
- Feedback Loop: Gather insights to inform future product development.
- Support the Sales process with product-specific expertise.

### Do's and Don'ts

- Do: Personalize demos and quantify the value of your solution.
- Do: Communicate value in terms of cost savings, improved efficiency, reduced risk.
- Do: Be prepared to answer technical questions. Do not be vague.
- Do: Get reference calls and show testimonials.
- Don't: Ignore procurement.
- Don't: Offer Proof of Concepts (POCs) or pilots.

### How to Prove Value is Best

**Solution Presentation Meeting:**
- Get the attendee list ahead of time.
- Review the presentation with the executive sponsor in advance.
- Interview all participants before the group presentation, if possible.
- Keep score. Gain agreement that you have successfully checked the box.
- Close with: "We require timely engagement and adherence to our joint evaluation plan."

**Gain Consensus and Triangulate Project Approval:**
- Ensure all evaluation participants have a voice and vote. This is critical to uncovering detractors.
- Triangulate project standing with business, IT, and finance.
- Does IT know how many resources to plan for?

**Prove Value is Best:**
- Gain alignment to your differentiation.
- Anticipate competitive positioning.
- Structure proposals to de-risk the project.
- Ensure your executive sponsor is well prepared to make the case for [COMPANY].
- People buy on cost, value, and risk. Which one does the client think you are?

### Moving to the Next Stage

**Teach/Prove Exit Criteria:**
- Selected.
- PSE added to Chatter.
- Opportunity submitted for approval.
- Close Plan agreed by sponsor.
- Submitted Contract Request.
- Met with Procurement.
- Value confirmed.

**Qualify Out Exit Activities:**
- Close/Lost Analysis with guidance to Product, Marketing, Business, and Competitive Intelligence.

## Stage 40: Best Practice Demo

### Before the Demo

- Identify the top 3-5 things the customer is interested in (prepare and show top-down).
- Run proper discovery first. The first discovery should be no more than 30 minutes.
- Understand the audience and tailor the demo to their specific needs.
- Plan and rehearse the demo. Prepare visual aids and rehearse the flow.
- Test the software before the demo. Troubleshoot any issues upfront.
- Gather resources: customer testimonials, case studies, competitive information.
- Prepare for questions. Know the software's capabilities and limitations.
- Send a pre-meeting email with the agenda and recording/webcam expectations.

### During the Demo

- Use Tell-Show-Tell. Start with the most important. Avoid unnecessary information.
- Use real-world examples and scenarios.
- Encourage audience participation and interaction.
- Be prepared to answer technical questions.
- Speak in first person using persona names. (Example: "I'm Kevin, I do, I execute." Avoid "you can" or "you should.")
- Use the ASD to take notes, manage timing, and questions in the chat.
- Pause and ask questions to keep people's attention.
- Take breaks at least every 45 minutes. Use a break timer.
- Use your webcam and ask people to join you.
- Give at least 10 minutes at the end for Q&A, mutual action plan, and next steps.

### After the Demo

- Call all participants individually to ask about their opinion: what was good, what is missing. Done by the ASD, one by one.
- Share the recording and materials via Paperflite.
- Answer questions raised but not answered during the demo.
- Schedule a follow-up call or meeting to discuss next steps.

### Best Practice Webcam Tips

- Maintain eye contact with the camera to build trust.
- Use open palm gestures.
- Position yourself in the center of the frame.
- Slow down movements.
- Use a solid-colored background.
- Dress professionally.
- Ensure contrast between your clothing, hair, and background.
- Avoid black clothing, stripes, and checks.
- Do a tech rehearsal before the session.
- Don't read from a script. Put it in your own words.
- Join the meeting early.
- Use a microphone positioned four finger-widths from your mouth.
- Eliminate light coming from behind your background.
- Ideal camera distance for relationship building: 1 to 2.5 feet.

### Demo Preparation Steps

1. Use the RSP to get the latest Presentation and Demo Content.
2. Prepare the demo and do a trial run.
3. Reference Confluence for Demo Prep issues and how-to guides.
4. Check existing demos from other SCs on GoConsensus or the account folder.

### Demo Recording and Upload to Paperflite

1. Record the session. Best if you or the ASD is the host.
2. Upload to GoConsensus as a single experience.
3. Add the link to Paperflite.

## RFX Process

### Overview

The primary goal of the RFP Response stage is to prove your value is best and qualify hard on whether to participate.

### Responsibilities

**RFP Team:** Answer sections related to corporate, financial, HR, security, support, training, and implementation methodology.

**Sales:**
- Actively qualify and thoroughly review the RFP.
- Be proactive: reach out to the prospect before submitting a response.
- Responsible for: cover letter, management summary, references, pricing, commercial decisions, competitive/strategic sections.
- Change the process: try to broaden the scope, request discovery meetings, or seek additional information.
- Price strategically: avoid using list prices in a blind RFP.
- Develop a tailored response.
- Work closely with PreSales, PS, and Product Management.

**PreSales:**
- Actively qualify and review the RFP.
- Responsible for: functional, application technology, and implementation/project plan sections.
- Create tailored product demonstrations.
- Provide deep technical expertise.
- Collaborate with Product Management.

**Professional Services:**
- Provide insights on implementation timelines and deliverables.
- Collaborate with Sales and PreSales on a comprehensive implementation plan.

**Product Management:**
- Collaborate on tailored solutions.
- Provide product-specific insights.
- Gather feedback to inform future product development.

### RFP Process Steps

1. Meet with ASD for a high-level check on whether to participate. Use the OSD to summarize your analysis.
2. Follow the RFP process SOP.
3. Send back the Proposal and Demo.

### RFP Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Actively qualify whether to participate | Blindly respond |
| Try to change the process | Allow procurement to limit access |
| Ask for a meeting with SVP, AVP, etc. | Cut and paste from someone else's RFP |
| Require discovery before submission | Use list prices |
| Get the list of competitors | |
| Call anyone and everyone, even if told not to | |
| Personalize your response | |
| Push back on short turnaround timetables | |

### Best Practice for RFP Answering

**Discovery:** Identify the project scope, budget, and goals. Research the prospect's industry and pain points. Ask clarifying questions.

**Qualify:** Assess whether it's worth pursuing. Analyze the potential ROI, the likelihood of winning, and your ability to meet requirements. Involve key stakeholders.

**Draft and Issue:** Gather relevant information about your software. Address the RFP's specific requirements. Proofread and edit the proposal before submitting.

**Demo:** Prepare a customized product demonstration. Practice and be prepared to answer technical questions. Follow up with the prospect after the demo.

**Shortlist:** Be responsive and proactive. Provide additional information as requested. Demonstrate flexibility and a willingness to collaborate.

**Negotiation:** Collaborate with the client on contract terms. Clearly communicate expectations and deliverables. Be prepared to compromise and find creative solutions.

## Stage 50: Negotiation

### Goal
Finalize the deal. Capture a fair share of the value. Negotiate business and legal terms.

### Key Responsibilities

**Sales:**
- Recognize that you are always negotiating. Be mindful of communication from day one.
- Organize Procurement Meetings and Contract redlines.
- Transition from Evaluation Plan to Close Plan.
- Always ask "why" when presented with objections.
- Build rapport and trust with the customer.
- Leverage data and analytics to support your pricing and value propositions.
- Prepare for objections.

**Pre-Sales:**
- Handover to Professional Service using the OSD.
- Provide deep technical expertise.

**Prof. Services:**
- Create a Statement of Work (SOW).

**Legal:**
- Get contracts and Order forms signed (use DocuSign).
- Procurement Meetings and Contract redlines.

### Do's and Don'ts

- Do: Know when you're negotiating.
- Do: Let them make a counteroffer first. Don't negotiate with yourself.
- Do: Test for procurement "pound of flesh."
- Do: Negotiate contract T&C and price together.
- Do: Collect all objections before solving any.
- Do: Track Give/Get.
- Do: Ask "why." Get to interests, not just objections.
- Do: Get management approval before agreeing to any price or terms.
- Do: Follow the company's process. Customer signs first, then your company.
- Don't: Get out over your skis without management cover.
- Don't: Use their paper.

### How to Get the Best Deal

**Our primary goal:** Contract a fair exchange of value.
**Our secondary goal:** Properly forecast when the customer will sign.

**Procurement Meetings:**
- Involve procurement early. They are your friend.
- Schedule multiple calendar events in advance to hold the customer accountable.
- Price extended assumes use of [COMPANY]'s paper.

**Negotiation Tracker (Give-Get):**
- Allows you to track concessions and ensure a fair and balanced negotiation.

**Close Plan Questions:**
- Is a Purchase Order required?
- How many approvers are in the PO process?
- Who's budget? How much OPEX or CAPEX?
- Who will approve?
- Who has to sign and when?
- When are they on vacation?
- What happens if they don't sign?

### Standard Commercial Terms

- Lead with minimum 3-year, non-cancellable terms for convenience subscription.
- Annual subscription fees are due in full in advance at the start date of the subscription term.
- Pricing assumes use of [COMPANY]'s MSA, OF, and SLA.
- SLA can be upgraded to Level 2 or 3 for an additional fee.
- Professional services will be delivered on a T&M basis.
- Always quote annual upgrade for single-tenant solutions.
- When cross-selling, position to extend existing products to the same term as the new product sale.
- If you have to use the client's MSA, always get a concession in return. Always use our Order Form.

- Commercials signed.

- MSA, OF, SLA, SOW signed by customer.
