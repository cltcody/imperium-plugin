# Command Center Business — When to Use What

A situational map: *"I'm trying to do X → reach for Y."* For the full catalogue see
[`INVENTORY.md`](INVENTORY.md). Skills are invoked by description — just say what you
want (e.g. *"stress-test this plan"* → `grill-me`); if a skill doesn't trigger, ask for
it **by name**.

---

## Documents & presentations

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Build a branded slide deck (PPTX, LinkedIn carousel) | `pptx-generator` |
| Write a branded Word document (proposal, summary, handover) | `docx-generator` |
| Architecture / flow / process diagram, decision tree | `diagram` |
| Map a company's supply chain visually | `supply-chain-map` |

## Communications

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Follow-up email or recap after a call | `field-comms-writer` |
| Raw meeting notes → actions, red flags, follow-up | `meeting-notes-structurer` |
| Text sounds AI-written — make it human | `humanize` |
| LinkedIn post, article, or Live script | `linkedin-post` |

## Meetings & conversations

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Prep a workshop agenda / multi-day EBC | `workshop-agenda-builder` |
| Prep for a single executive meeting | `exec-briefing-prep` |
| A pricing / scope / renewal negotiation ahead (build the brief) | `negotiation-prep` |
| Handle a tense conversation or objection, live | `tactical-empathy-coach` |

## Thinking & quality

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Stress-test a plan, doc, or idea before it ships | `grill-me` |
| Tag what's confirmed vs assumed before it leaves the team | `confidence-tagger` |
| Untangle a complex conflict or recurring problem | `toc-bbit-expert` |
| Write an SOP / runbook / escalation procedure | `sop-creator` |

## Customer research

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Research a company's business & supply chain | `supply-chain-map` |
| How do we compare to a named competitor | `competitive-battlecard` |

---

**Rule of thumb:** unsure which tool? Open **`find`** from the plugin menu and describe
what you're doing — it routes you. `guide` browses the full catalog interactively.
