# Benefits & Bureaucracy Navigator (US)

Turn a confusing government process into a clear checklist: the right agency, the forms, the
documents, the deadlines, the costs, and the traps. A workflow, not a council.

> **Frame:** orientation to save you time, **not legal, tax, or immigration advice.** Rules, forms,
> fees, and eligibility **change and vary by state** — always confirm on the **official .gov site**
> (or the agency directly) before relying on anything here, and see a licensed professional
> (attorney, CPA, accredited representative) for complex or high-stakes cases. Never enter SSNs or
> sensitive personal data into this chat.

## Step 1 — Identify the process & the agency

Clarify what the person is trying to do, then route to the responsible body (federal unless the
task is state-run):

| Need | Agency (official site) |
|------|------------------------|
| Retirement / disability (SSDI/SSI) / Medicare / SSN card | **Social Security Administration** — ssa.gov / medicare.gov |
| Federal taxes, refunds, transcripts, ID.me | **IRS** — irs.gov |
| Green card, citizenship, work authorization, visas | **USCIS** — uscis.gov (travel.state.gov for passports/visas) |
| Driver's license, state ID, vehicle registration, REAL ID | **State DMV** (varies by state) |
| Unemployment insurance, SNAP, TANF, state Medicaid | **Your state** labor / human-services agency |
| Health-insurance marketplace subsidies | **HealthCare.gov** (or your state exchange) |
| Veterans' benefits | **VA** — va.gov |
| Birth/marriage/death certificates | **State/county vital records** |

If it's genuinely unclear or spans agencies, say so and give the most likely one plus how to confirm.

## Step 2 — Requirements checklist

For the identified process, lay out:
- **Forms** — the specific form name/number (confirm the current version on the .gov site).
- **Documents** — what proof is needed (ID, income, residency, prior filings) — a gather-list.
- **Eligibility** — the general criteria and common disqualifiers (flag that thresholds change).
- **How to file** — online / mail / in-person / phone, and whether an appointment is needed.

## Step 3 — Timeline, cost, pitfalls

- **Deadlines & processing time** — filing windows, expected wait, expedite options.
- **Costs** — fees (and fee-waiver eligibility where it exists).
- **Common pitfalls** — the mistakes that cause rejections/delays (wrong form version, missing
  signature, expired documents, wrong office), and **scam warnings** (agencies don't demand
  gift-card payment or threaten arrest by phone).
- **Where to get real help** — free/official resources (SSA/IRS helplines, VITA for taxes,
  legal-aid, accredited immigration reps) rather than paid middlemen.
