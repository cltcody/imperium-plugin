# Competitive Battlecard

Fast, honest competitive positioning for live deals.
Know where we genuinely win, where they're strong, and how to handle their attacks.

---

## Step 1 — Tell the skill what you're working with

1. **Competitor name** — who are we up against?
2. **Products in scope**: ${user_config.product_a} | ${user_config.product_b} | ${user_config.product_c} | ${user_config.product_d} | Full ${user_config.suite_1}
3. **What has the customer said?** — any comments about the competitor, demo requests, pricing mentions
4. **Deal stage** — already in a competitive evaluation, or just preparing?
5. **Primary evaluation contact** — their role (IT, compliance, commercial, C-suite)

If the Knowledge base is connected: pull the latest battlecard for this competitor.
If Salesforce is connected: check for prior wins and losses against this competitor in similar accounts.

---

## Step 2 — Where we genuinely win

List 3–5 defensible, provable strengths versus this competitor for the products in scope.

Rules:
- Each strength needs proof — a demo capability, a customer reference, or a real metric
- Do NOT list things the competitor also has ("we have AI too")
- Focus on *depth*, *integration quality*, and *measurable outcome* — not checkbox features
- Be honest: overclaming here destroys credibility when the customer does their own research
- Confidence-tag every claim

```
WHERE WE WIN — ${user_config.company} vs [Competitor] — [Product in scope]

1. [Strength]
   Why it matters: [One sentence on the business outcome this delivers]
   Proof: [Reference customer / demo capability / measurable result]
   Confidence: 🟢 Confirmed / 🟡 Inferred

2. [Strength]
   Why it matters: [...]
   Proof: [...]
   Confidence: 🟢 / 🟡

3. [Strength]
   Why it matters: [...]
   Proof: [...]
   Confidence: 🟢 / 🟡
```

## Step 3 — Where they compete (their strongest claims)

What will the competitor say they're good at? Name their real strengths honestly.
If you pretend they're weak when they're not, you lose credibility when the customer finds out.

```
THEIR STRONGEST CLAIMS

1. [Their genuine strength or claim]
   How to respond: [Our counter — factual, not defensive, not dismissive]
   What to ask next: [A question that shifts the ground without attacking directly]

2. [Their genuine strength or claim]
   How to respond: [...]
   What to ask next: [...]

3. [Their genuine strength or claim]
   How to respond: [...]
   What to ask next: [...]
```

Attack: [What they'll say about us — be realistic]

## Step 5 — Discovery questions when a competitor is in play

Use these to understand where the competitor stands and find gaps in their solution —
without ever directly attacking them.

```
COMPETITIVE DISCOVERY QUESTIONS

"What did [Competitor] show you that you found most compelling?"
→ Learn what the customer values most — and where to go deeper

"Is there anything they showed you that raised questions or felt unclear?"
→ Surface gaps without attacking directly

"What are the top three criteria you're using to make this decision?"
→ Find out if the evaluation criteria favour us or them — and if not, change them

"Who on your side is driving the evaluation — and are all the stakeholders aligned?"
→ Identify internal champions for each vendor

"Have you spoken to any of their reference customers?"
→ Test how deep their validation has gone

"What would a failed implementation look like for you — what's the risk you're managing?"
→ Gets at the deeper criteria beyond features and price
```

## Step 6 — Deal strategy recommendation

Based on the above, here is the recommended approach:

```
COMPETITIVE DEAL STRATEGY

Competitor: [Name] | Stage: [Where we are in the deal]
Our position: Ahead / Behind / Even [be honest]

Priority action 1: [The single highest-leverage thing to do right now]
Priority action 2: [Second priority]
Priority action 3: [Third priority]

The trap to set:
[A demo moment, question, or reference that advantages us specifically against this competitor]

The landmine to defuse:
[Their biggest strength — get in front of it proactively rather than waiting for the customer to raise it]
```

---

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Strengths are honest — each has a proof point, not wishful thinking
- [ ] Competitor's strengths are acknowledged — no underestimating
- [ ] Attacks and responses are factual, not emotional
- [ ] Discovery questions are open-ended — not leading
- [ ] Deal strategy has one clear priority action — not a list of ten
- [ ] All claims confidence-tagged (🟢 Confirmed / 🟡 Inferred / 🔴 Unknown)
