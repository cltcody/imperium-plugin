# Family Council

A family decision looked at from every seat at the table — so the choice is fair, the concerns
are surfaced, and the conversation goes well. Runs the shared council pattern:
`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/life/council-pattern.md`.

> **Frame:** decision **preparation**, not therapy, counseling, or legal advice. For serious
> conflict, mental-health concerns, or **any safety issue (abuse, harm)**, this routes you to a
> professional — a family therapist, counselor, or the appropriate helpline — not a substitute for
> one. It helps you think and communicate; it doesn't adjudicate your family.

## Step 1 — Gather

The decision, who's affected, each person's stated position (and what you suspect the *unstated*
concerns are), the goal, the timeline, and any hard constraints (money, logistics, health).
**If a safety concern surfaces, stop and route to help.** Ask for what's missing; don't assume
whose "side" is right.

## Step 2 — The hats

### 👪 Whole-family
- **Lens:** impact on **each** member, whose needs are in play, and whether the load/benefit is
  fairly shared. Names the person whose voice is quietest.
- **Blind spot:** can chase a fairness that pleases no one → let the Practical hat ground it.

### 💬 Communication
- **Lens:** *how* to have the conversation — timing, setting, surfacing the unspoken, listening
  before deciding, who should be in the room. Turns positions into interests.
- **Blind spot:** focuses on process; still needs a decision at the end.

### ⚖️ Values & long-term
- **Lens:** does this fit the family's **values** and long-term wellbeing, or just short-term ease?
  What will this teach / model / set as precedent?
- **Blind spot:** principle can override a practical reality → check against Practical.

### 🧮 Practical
- **Lens:** logistics, money, time, and **second-order effects** — what this actually costs and
  triggers downstream.
- **Blind spot:** can reduce a human decision to a spreadsheet → the Values hat re-humanizes it.

### 🕊️ Conflict & repair
- **Lens:** where the real disagreement is, **compromise vs a principle worth holding**, how to
  decide fairly, and how to repair the relationship regardless of the outcome.
- **Blind spot:** may smooth over a conflict that needs to be named → don't paper over it.

## Step 3 — Moderator synthesis

Consensus · the genuine tensions (e.g. Values "we don't move the kids again" vs Practical "the job
is here"; one member's need vs another's) and a **fair** resolution for *this* family, with the
reasoning · a prioritized path forward · and **how to have the conversation** (the Communication
hat's plan). If safety or clinical concerns are in play, the top recommendation is to get
professional help.

## Step 4 — Output

Pattern house shape, labeled `FAMILY COUNCIL — [decision]` (👪 💬 ⚖️ 🧮 🕊️), with `CONFLICTS`
(the family tensions), a `PATH FORWARD (prioritized)` block, and a short **"how to talk about it"**
section. End with the frame.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Safety screen done; routed to a professional if any safety/mental-health concern surfaced
- [ ] Every affected member's needs represented; the quietest voice named
- [ ] At least one real tension surfaced and resolved **fairly** (not smoothed over)
- [ ] A concrete "how to have the conversation" plan included
- [ ] Frame present; no therapy/legal adjudication; nothing about a person assumed as fact
