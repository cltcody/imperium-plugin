# Insurance Review (US)

Check whether you're **correctly** insured — enough where it matters, not paying for what it
doesn't. A workflow, not a council. The guiding principle: **insure what you can't afford to
lose; self-insure the small stuff.**

> **Frame:** orientation, not insurance advice. Coverage terms, limits, and state minimums vary —
> confirm against your own policies and with a licensed agent/broker before changing anything.
> Never drop a real protection to save a little.

## Step 1 — Inventory

For each policy: **type · carrier · coverage limits · deductible · premium · renewal date**.
Cover the six that matter:
- **Health** — plan type, deductible, out-of-pocket max, network, HSA-eligible?
- **Auto** — liability limits (bodily injury / property), **uninsured-motorist**, collision/comp, deductible.
- **Home / Renters** — dwelling & personal-property limits, **liability**, deductible, replacement-cost vs actual-cash-value, flood/quake (usually separate!).
- **Life** — term vs permanent, coverage amount, who depends on the income.
- **Disability** — short/long-term, own-occupation?, benefit %, elimination period (**the most commonly missing coverage**).
- **Umbrella** — extra liability above auto/home limits.

## Step 2 — Assess (what to check per policy)

1. **Adequacy** — are limits sized to the **assets and income at risk**? (Liability especially:
   a lawsuit can exceed auto/home limits → that's what umbrella is for.)
2. **Gaps** — no disability coverage; no umbrella with meaningful assets; term life with
   dependents and none; low uninsured-motorist; no flood coverage in a flood zone; ACV instead of
   replacement cost.
3. **Overlaps / waste** — duplicate coverage (credit-card rental-car + auto add-on), **deductibles
   set so low the premium costs more than the risk**, extended warranties, add-ons you'd never claim.
4. **Deductible ↔ premium** — raising a deductible you could comfortably cover often beats a low
   one; match the deductible to your emergency fund.

Flag each: 🟥 gap / under-insured · 🟧 review / re-price · 🟩 right-sized.

## Step 3 — Prioritized action plan

Sort by **protection per dollar**, gaps first:
- **Close a gap** — add the missing essential coverage (disability, umbrella, adequate liability).
- **Re-size** — adjust limits/deductibles to fit assets + emergency fund.
- **Cut waste** — drop duplicates and low-value add-ons; re-shop over-priced policies.

Per action: **what to do · rough cost/impact · why it matters.**

## Quality checklist

- [ ] All six lines reviewed; each with limits, deductible, premium, renewal
- [ ] Liability sized to assets/income at risk; umbrella considered
- [ ] **Disability** and **term life (if dependents)** explicitly checked — the usual gaps
- [ ] Waste/duplicates and deductible-vs-premium addressed
- [ ] Gaps prioritized above savings; nothing binding asserted — "confirm with a licensed agent"
