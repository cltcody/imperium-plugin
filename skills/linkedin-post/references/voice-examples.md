# Voice examples

Before/after rewrites that show the humanization moves in each voice. Examples teach voice
faster than rules do. When drafting, prefer the **after** column's instincts: cut the opener,
drop the hype, name the specific, commit to one edge.

Use these as direction, not templates to copy. Do not lift the example metrics as if they were
real facts — swap in the user's real numbers, or ask.

## company (branded / product voice)

Voice: direct, specific, confident, no hype. Names the product (`[COMPANY_PRODUCT]`) and makes an
honest ask. No unfounded outcome promises ("guaranteed", "10x overnight").

| Generic AI line | Human rewrite | Why it's better |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| "We're excited to share that [COMPANY_PRODUCT] is launching soon! 🚀" | "The thing that used to take our customers three days now takes an afternoon. That's what [COMPANY_PRODUCT] ships next month." | Opens on the concrete before/after, not the announcement. |
| "Our platform leverages cutting-edge AI to streamline your workflow. 💡" | "It flags the five records most likely to be wrong before you file. Not magic — just the checks your best analyst already runs, automatically." | Drops hype words, names the actual mechanism and a real number. |
| "Digital transformation is more important than ever for modern business. ✅" | "Most teams don't have a data problem. They have a 'nobody trusts the dashboard' problem — and those are fixed differently." | Replaces consensus filler with a sharp, arguable claim. |
| "Book a demo today and transform your operations! 🎯" | "If you close your books manually and it hurts every month, the demo is 20 minutes. worth a look." | Honest CTA, names the real reader, no transformation promise. |

## personal (own voice, no brand)

Voice: practitioner, direct, generous, specific, your own English variant. One defensible
opinion per piece. No pitch — share because it's worth saying.

| Generic AI line | Human rewrite | Why it's better |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| "In today's fast-paced sales environment, discovery is more important than ever." | "Most discovery calls fail in the first four minutes, before anyone asks a real question." | Cuts the throat-clearing, opens on a specific, arguable claim. |
| "Here are 3 key insights to improve your demos: ..." | "I cut one slide from a demo last quarter and the deal closed faster. The slide was our company timeline." | Replaces the triad with one real, specific anecdote and a point. |
| "MEDDPICC is a powerful framework that can transform your qualification." | "MEDDPICC won't save a deal with no Champion. It just tells you sooner that you're wasting your time." | Drops hype, states a contrarian truth practitioners feel. |
| "Pro Tip: Always ask open-ended questions! 💡" | "The best question I know is boring: 'Walk me through how you do this today.' It outperforms every clever one." | Kills the listicle lead-in, names the actual technique with a claim. |
| "What are your thoughts? Let me know in the comments! 👇" | "I've stopped opening demos with agendas. I think they lower the energy. Tell me I'm wrong." | Swaps engagement-bait for a real opinion that earns genuine replies. |
| "Solution consultants play a vital role in the sales process." | "The SC is the only person in the room the buyer assumes isn't lying. That's the whole job." | Replaces a platitude with a sharp, memorable framing of the role. |
