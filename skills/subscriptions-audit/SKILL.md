# Subscriptions & Recurring-Cost Audit (US)

Bring order to your recurring spend and find the fastest cuts — without dropping coverage you
actually need. A workflow, not a council.

> **Frame:** orientation and market comparison, not legal or insurance advice. Confirm exact
> terms and cancellation windows against your own account/contract before acting.

## Step 1 — Inventory

List every recurring charge (ask the user, or offer to reason from a pasted bank/card statement).
Typical categories:
- **Streaming & content** — video, music, news, audiobooks, games.
- **Software & cloud** — apps, storage, AI tools, password managers, domains/hosting.
- **Memberships** — gym, warehouse clubs, professional orgs, loyalty/"plus" tiers.
- **Telecom & utilities** — phone, internet, extra lines, add-ons.
- **Insurance** — health, auto, home/renters, life, umbrella, device/extended warranties.
- **Financial** — account/card annual fees, advisory %, overdraft/"protection" add-ons.

For each, capture what you can: **provider · what it does · $/month · $/year · billing cycle ·
next renewal date · cancellation window**.

## Step 2 — Assess each charge (four questions)

1. **Do I still use it?** — used vs forgotten; **duplicates** (two cloud plans, overlapping
   streaming, phone insurance that duplicates a card benefit).
2. **Is the price-to-value right?** — rough market comparison; overpriced legacy plans; a cheaper
   tier that covers 90%; annual vs monthly savings.
3. **Am I correctly covered?** — take **insurance gaps** (no umbrella, thin liability, no
   disability) as seriously as over-insurance. Cutting a real protection to save $10 is a bad trade.
4. **When can I get out?** — next renewal, notice window, and **US traps**: auto-renewal after a
   free trial, annual plans that renew silently, "dark-pattern" cancellation flows, and that
   auto-renew disclosure/refund rules **vary by state** — confirm your state and the actual terms.

Flag each: 🟥 cut/expensive · 🟧 review/negotiate · 🟩 keep.

## Step 3 — Prioritized action plan

Sort by **savings per effort**:
- **Quick wins** — cancel/downgrade now, clear savings (forgotten apps, a trial about to renew,
  an overpriced plan with a cheaper tier).
- **Mid-term** — note the renewal/notice date; negotiate or switch before it hits.
- **Close a gap** — add missing essential coverage (often more important than a cut).

Per action: **what to do · by when (the deadline!) · estimated $/year saved.** Sum the total.

## Step 4 — Output + template

```
SUBSCRIPTIONS & RECURRING COSTS — Audit
════════════════════════════════════════════
Reviewed: [n] items · Total: ~$[X]/year

FINDINGS
🟥 [item]  $[/yr]  → [action]  (by: [date])  saves ~$[X]
🟧 [item]  ...
🟩 [item]  keep

GAPS
- [e.g. no umbrella liability] → [recommendation]

ACTION PLAN
Quick wins:  1. …  2. …
Mid-term:    1. … (cancel/switch by [date])
ESTIMATED ANNUAL SAVINGS: ~$[X]/year
```

On request, generate a **cancellation / downgrade message** (provider, account number, request to
cancel effective the next renewal, ask for written confirmation, and — where state law allows —
a prorated refund).

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Every item has $/year and its next renewal/cancellation date
- [ ] Duplicates and forgotten/trial charges called out explicitly
- [ ] Insurance **gaps** flagged as seriously as savings
- [ ] Each action has a deadline and an estimated saving; total summed
- [ ] Cancellation windows marked "confirm on your account/state", not asserted as fact
