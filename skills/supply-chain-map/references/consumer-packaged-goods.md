# Consumer Packaged Goods — Sector Reference for Supply Chain Mapping

**Cluster:** Consumer
**Representative Companies:** P&G, Philip Morris International (PMI), n7, Smurfit WestRock (packaging), Unilever, Henkel, Kimberly-Clark, Colgate-Palmolive
**Taxonomy description:** Products that go onto or near human bodies to support daily operations

---

## Typical Make/Buy Split
- Large CPG (P&G, Unilever): 65–80% in-house manufacturing; 20–35% CMO for regional/seasonal/tail
- Beauty and personal care (subset): 70–85% in-house for core; CMO for launches and tail
- Tobacco (PMI): 85–95% own manufacturing; strict regulatory control limits CMO use
- Packaging specialists (Smurfit WestRock): vertically integrated — own pulp, paper, board, conversion
- Key signal: "third-party manufacturers", "co-manufacturers", "co-packers" in 10-K risk factors

## Inbound Raw Materials — Typical Modal Split
| Material | Mode | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Petrochemical-derived resins | Pipeline or Road FTL | Plastics feedstock; regional suppliers preferred |
| Paper / pulp / board | Rail or Road FTL | High volume; weight-intensive |
| Chemical actives / surfactants | Road FTL | EU-sourced typically |
| Fragrance / flavour compounds | Road FTL or Air | Low volume, high value |
| Natural botanicals / plant extracts | Ocean LCL | Exotic volumes; ethically sensitive |
| Glass bottles | Road FTL (EU-EU) or Ocean FCL (Asia→EU) | Heavy, low value density → ocean preferred |
| Plastic packaging components | Road FTL or Ocean FCL | |
| Metals (aerosol cans, aluminium) | Road FTL | Regional metal packaging converters |
| Cartons / secondary packaging | Road FTL | Usually local regional supplier |

## Distribution Norms
- Hub-and-spoke dominant: pan-regional DC (NL, BE, DE for EU; OH/NJ/TX for US) + local sub-DCs
- Travel retail / duty-free: bonded warehouse near major airports (AMS, DXB, SIN)
- 3PL widespread: own DC for core volume markets; 3PL for tail / emerging markets
- APAC: Shanghai + Singapore/HK hub for SE Asia
- Shelf-ready packaging (SRP): retail-ready units require specific DC handling
- Route-to-market complexity: direct in large modern trade; through distributor for traditional trade / EM

## Outbound Modal Split
| Channel | Mode | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Hypermarket / mass retail | Road FTL | Full pallets, regular cadence |
| Pharmacy / drug chains | Road FTL or LTL | Mixed SKU, weekly replenishment |
| Convenience / impulse | Road LTL | Small format, frequent drops |
| Travel retail / duty-free | Ocean or Air | Consolidated, duty-free bonded |
| Ecom / DTC | Parcel (B2C express) | Single-unit, growing rapidly |
| Ecom marketplace (CN) | Parcel last-mile | Cainiao, SF Express typical |
| Wholesale / distributors (EM) | Ocean FCL | Export markets, large volume |

## Air Freight Use Cases (declining sector-wide)
- Launch support: new product, insufficient lead time for ocean
- Emergency replenishment: stockout or viral demand spike (social media-driven)
- Travel retail urgency: airport restocks before peak travel season
- Sector target: most large CPG companies targeting <3% of shipments by air by 2027

## Top 10 Use Cases (Business-Driven)
1. **Demand Spike Absorption** — Absorb a 20% demand spike (viral/social or seasonal) without stockouts and without air freight escalation
2. **Model & React to Disruption** — Model and re-route production and distribution within 4 hours of plant or DC disruption to protect shelf availability
3. **Gross Margin Stability** — Keep gross margin stable within ±1% irrespective of raw material price fluctuation through hedging and formulation optimisation
4. **Working Capital Leadership** — Maximise working capital by leading inventory turns across the network while maintaining >98% case fill rate
5. **Launch on Time** — Launch new SKU simultaneously across all key markets within <2-week variance, with zero out-of-stocks at first shipment
6. **Revenue at Risk** — Keep revenue at risk from supply disruptions below 2% of quarterly revenue across top-50 SKUs
7. **Air Freight Elimination** — Reduce air freight to <2% of total outbound shipments through better demand sensing and DC inventory positioning
8. **Retail On-Shelf Availability** — Achieve >98.5% on-shelf availability at top-10 retail customers, reducing phantom inventory and OSA gaps
9. **Retailer Collaboration Efficiency** — Automate >80% of retailer replenishment signals through EDI/CPFR integration, reducing manual order management
10. **Sustainable Packaging Compliance** — Achieve 100% recyclable or reusable packaging across portfolio by committed year with full supply chain traceability

## Regulatory & Compliance Considerations
- Labelling regulations: country-specific ingredient, language, and claims requirements (EU, FDA, local)
- Tobacco-specific (PMI): plain packaging laws, track-and-trace (EU TPD Article 15), illicit trade controls
- Chemical regulations: REACH (EU), EPA (US) for chemical actives and preservatives
- Plastic packaging taxes: UK Plastic Packaging Tax, EU Packaging Regulation — drives recycled content sourcing
- Consumer safety: product recall protocols; CPSC (US), RAPEX (EU) notification requirements

## Sustainability Signals to Watch For
- Packaging recyclability and recycled content % (vs commitments)
- Air freight % of total shipments (vs baseline and target)
- Scope 3 transport and packaging emissions
- Palm oil, mica, and other ethically-sensitive raw material certifications (RSPO, Rainforest Alliance)
- Water usage and wastewater treatment at manufacturing sites
