# Food & Beverage — Sector Reference for Supply Chain Mapping

**Cluster:** Consumer
**Representative Companies:** Campbell's, Mars, Kellanova, Nestlé, AB InBev, Mondelez, Tyson Foods, Danone
**Taxonomy description:** Products destined for human digestive tracts

---

## Typical Make/Buy Split
- Large F&B (Nestlé, Mars): 60–80% own plants; 20–40% co-packers / co-manufacturers
- Private label / retailer brand-led: 90%+ co-packer
- Beverages: higher own-plant percentage (60–90%) due to fill-to-order economics and freshness
- Co-packer role: regional demand surge, seasonal overflow, format extensions (new pack sizes)
- Key signal: "co-manufacturer", "co-packer", "toll manufacturing" in 10-K or investor presentations

## Inbound Raw Materials — Typical Modal Split
| Material | Mode | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Agricultural commodities (grain, cocoa, sugar) | Ocean bulk / Rail | Very high volume, low value density |
| Dairy / fresh ingredients | Road (short-haul, chilled) | Perishable; regional sourcing mandatory |
| Meat / protein | Road (refrigerated) | Short shelf-life; kills near feedlot |
| Packaging (cans, glass, PET bottles) | Road FTL | Usually local/regional to reduce breakage |
| Flavours / extracts / additives | Road FTL or courier | Low volume, high value; specialty suppliers |
| Oils / fats (palm, sunflower, soy) | Ocean FCL (tanker) or Road | Large volumes; commodity |
| Coffee / tea (green/black) | Ocean FCL | Long-haul from origin; stored in silos |
| Wine / spirits base | Ocean FCL or Road | Country-of-origin traceability required |
| Water | Local utility | — |

## Distribution Norms
- Very regional model: heavy weight, perishability, and low margin drive regional distribution
- Ambient grocery: DC → retailer DC → store (2-step); or direct-to-retailer DC (modern trade)
- Fresh / chilled: direct-to-store (DSD) or short-cycle temperature-controlled DC
- Beverages: often self-operated fleet (high frequency, dedicated vehicles)
- Cold chain: mandatory for dairy, meat, ready-to-eat (RTE), and frozen products
- Seasonal peaks: confectionery (Q4/Valentine/Easter); ice cream (Q2/Q3); hot drinks (Q4)

## Outbound Modal Split
| Channel | Mode | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Grocery / supermarket | Road FTL | High frequency, ambient or chilled |
| Convenience / impulse | Road LTL or DSD | Small drop sizes, frequent |
| Foodservice / HoReCa | Road LTL | Mixed ambient + chilled |
| Export (shelf-stable) | Ocean FCL | Long shelf life products |
| Ecom / meal kits | Parcel + cold pack | Growing; high fulfilment cost |
| Club / wholesale (Costco) | Road FTL | High volume, pallet-in/pallet-out |

## Air Freight Use Cases (near-zero; exception signals bad planning)
- Luxury / premium: fresh truffles, live lobster, premium wine for high-end hospitality
- Emergency recall replacement: production failure or food safety recall replacement supply
- Rule of thumb: if a mainstream F&B product flies, it's either premium or broken planning

## Top 10 Use Cases (Business-Driven)
1. **Demand Spike Absorption** — Absorb a 25% seasonal demand spike without production overruns, excess cold chain costs, or out-of-stocks
2. **Gross Margin in Commodity Volatility** — Keep gross margin stable within ±1% irrespective of cocoa, grain, or dairy price fluctuation through procurement hedging and formulation agility
3. **Working Capital on Perishables** — Reduce days of fresh ingredient inventory by 20% while maintaining >99% production schedule adherence
4. **Retailer On-Shelf Availability** — Achieve >98.5% on-shelf availability across top-10 grocery customers, reducing out-of-stocks and lost sales
5. **Model & React to Food Safety Event** — Execute a voluntary product recall from shelf within 24 hours of safety decision, with 100% lot traceability to consumer level
6. **Launch on Time** — Launch new product or pack size across all markets within <2-week variance, with full cold/ambient chain set up at launch
7. **Revenue at Risk from Harvest Failure** — Keep revenue at risk from single-origin agricultural ingredient shortfall below 3% of annual revenue through dual sourcing
8. **Cold Chain Compliance** — Achieve 100% temperature compliance across the cold chain with real-time alerts and automated corrective action
9. **Co-packer Integration** — Achieve parity in production schedule visibility and quality compliance between own plants and top-10 co-packers
10. **DSD Route Efficiency** — Reduce direct store delivery cost-per-drop by 15% through route optimisation and frequency rationalisation

## Regulatory & Compliance Considerations
- Food safety: FSMA (US Food Safety Modernization Act), EU Food Law Regulation, HACCP mandatory
- Labelling: FDA/FSIS (US), EU FIC — allergens, nutrition facts, country of origin mandatory
- Cold chain compliance: HACCP temperature records required; GDP equivalent for food (GFSI standards)
- Agricultural imports: phytosanitary certificates, country-of-origin documentation, tariff rate quotas
- Organic / fair trade certification: traceability requirements throughout supply chain
- Recall procedures: FDA/FSIS mandatory recall notification; RASFF (EU) rapid alert system

## Sustainability Signals to Watch For
- Food waste reduction targets (% of production and distribution waste)
- Agricultural sourcing certifications (Rainforest Alliance, RSPO, UTZ for palm/cocoa)
- Cold chain energy consumption and refrigerant transition (F-gas phase-out)
- Packaging recyclability commitments and recycled content %
- Scope 3 Category 1 (agricultural raw materials — largest Scope 3 for most F&B companies)
- Water intensity at manufacturing sites
