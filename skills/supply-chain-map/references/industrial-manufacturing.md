# Industrial Manufacturing — Sector Reference for Supply Chain Mapping

**Cluster:** Industrial
**Representative Companies:** Caterpillar (CAT), Assa Abloy, Emerson, Honeywell, Parker Hannifin, Rockwell Automation, Siemens Industrial, Schneider Electric
**Taxonomy description:** Specialized and/or big and/or complicated lumps of metal, sold to businesses & consumers

---

## Typical Make/Buy Split
- Large capital equipment (CAT model): 50–70% in-house; 30–50% outsourced sub-assemblies and components
- Specialist products (Assa Abloy locks): 60–80% in-house across owned brands; platform acquisitions add further plants
- Electronics / controls sub-systems: typically outsourced to specialist EMS or Tier 1 sub-suppliers
- Castings and forgings: often outsourced (foundries, forge shops) but with proprietary tooling
- Key signal: "vertically integrated", "manufacturing footprint", "lean manufacturing", "preferred supplier programme" in annual reports

## Inbound Raw Materials — Typical Modal Split
| Material | Mode | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Steel (plate, bar, tube) | Rail or Road FTL | Very high weight; ocean for intercontinental |
| Castings / forgings | Road FTL or Ocean FCL | Heavy; usually regional foundry |
| Hydraulics / pneumatics | Road FTL | Sub-assembly from specialist Tier 1 |
| Electronics / PLCs / controls | Air or Road | High value, time-sensitive; EMS-sourced |
| Engineered plastics | Road FTL | Regional compounders |
| Bearings / seals | Road FTL or Ocean FCL | Often single-source specialist (SKF, Schaeffler) |
| Fasteners / standard parts | Road FTL | MRO and production consumables — VMI common |

## Distribution Norms
- Make-to-order (MTO) or configure-to-order (CTO) dominant for complex products — low finished goods inventory
- Make-to-stock (MTS) for high-volume standard product lines and spare parts
- Dealer / distributor network for mid-size equipment (construction, agriculture); direct for large capital
- Spare parts / aftermarket: forward-stocking at regional DCs; critical parts at dealer locations
- Project logistics for large capital equipment: over-dimensional (OOD) road permits; special handling
- Export crating: heavy-duty wooden crating for large equipment — specialist packers

## Outbound Modal Split
| Channel | Mode | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Large capital equipment | Road (OOD) or Rail or Ocean (breakbulk/RORO) | Complex permitting; specialist carriers |
| Mid-size equipment | Road FTL | Standard flatbed or lowboy |
| Spare parts (dealer) | Road LTL or parcel | High SKU count; fill rate critical |
| Spare parts (direct/urgent) | Air express | Machine downtime = production loss |
| Export (EM markets) | Ocean FCL (containerised) or breakbulk | Project logistics partnerships |

## Air Freight Use Cases
- Emergency production parts: customer machine downtime has explicit cost per hour — parts fly
- Electronic control modules: high value-density; shortage-sensitive
- New product samples: engineering validation parts for customer qualification
- Aftermarket critical parts: 24/7 uptime commitment drives structural air freight in aftermarket

## Top 10 Use Cases (Business-Driven)
1. **Machine Uptime / Parts Availability** — Achieve >98% spare parts fill rate within 24 hours to minimise customer machine downtime cost
2. **Demand Spike Absorption** — Absorb a 25% increase in equipment orders without extending lead time beyond contracted delivery window
3. **Aftermarket Revenue at Risk** — Keep aftermarket revenue at risk from parts stockouts below 2% of total annual aftermarket revenue
4. **Gross Margin Protection** — Keep product gross margin within ±2% despite steel and raw material price volatility
5. **New Product Launch on Time** — Bring new equipment model to first commercial delivery within <4-week variance of committed launch date
6. **Working Capital Optimisation** — Reduce raw material and WIP inventory by 15% through better MRP discipline without disrupting production schedule
7. **Model & React to Disruption** — Model and reroute production within 24 hours of a key casting or forging supplier failure
8. **Services Revenue Growth** — Grow connected equipment and predictive maintenance service attach rate from 20% to 50% of installed base
9. **Manufacturing Lead Time Reduction** — Cut average order-to-ship lead time by 20% through lean manufacturing and supplier schedule integration
10. **Export Compliance Efficiency** — Process all dual-use export authorisations with <48-hour approval cycle, zero compliance violations

## Regulatory & Compliance Considerations
- Export controls: dual-use equipment (EAR, EU dual-use regulation); end-user certificates required for controlled markets
- CE marking / UL certification: mandatory for market access in EU and US
- Safety standards: OSHA (US), Machinery Directive (EU); product liability drives design and sourcing decisions
- Environmental: RoHS for electronics content; REACH for chemical substances; EPA compliance for emissions
- Conflict minerals: industrial equipment uses significant 3TG metals — SEC/EU reporting

## Sustainability Signals to Watch For
- Product electrification commitments (% of revenue from electric/low-emission products)
- Manufacturing energy intensity (kWh per unit of output)
- Steel sustainability: recycled content %, green steel sourcing
- Waste and water reduction at manufacturing sites
- Scope 3 Cat 11 (use of sold products): emissions from equipment in operation
