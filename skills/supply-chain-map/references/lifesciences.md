# Lifesciences (Pharma & Biotech) — Sector Reference for Supply Chain Mapping

**Cluster:** Consumer
**Representative Companies:** Biogen, Eli Lilly, Abbott, Pfizer, Johnson & Johnson, Roche, Novo Nordisk, AstraZeneca
**Taxonomy description:** Products destined to go into human biological systems

---

## Typical Make/Buy Split
- Large pharma (Pfizer, J&J): 50–70% in-house API and formulation; 30–50% CDMO/CMO
- Biotechs (Biogen, Regeneron): higher CDMO dependency — biologics manufacturing requires specialist bioreactors
- Generics: 60–80% CMO-reliant (asset-light model common)
- Specialty / rare disease (Lilly, Novo): own API for strategic molecules; CMO for non-core and regional supply
- Key signal: "CDMO" (contract development and manufacturing organisation), "sole-source API", "qualified manufacturing site" in pipeline/regulatory filings

## Inbound Raw Materials — Typical Modal Split
| Material | Mode | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| API (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient) | Air (high value) or Ocean FCL | Temperature-controlled; CoA mandatory |
| Excipients (bulk) | Road FTL or Ocean FCL | Low value, high volume |
| Biological starting materials (cell banks, fermentation media) | Air (validated, cryogenic) | Ultra-cold chain (-196°C for cell banks) |
| Primary packaging (vials, syringes, blister) | Road FTL (EU) or Ocean FCL (Asia) | Pharma-grade glass — limited suppliers |
| Secondary packaging | Road FTL | Usually local/regional |
| Adjuvants / excipients (specialty) | Air or Road | Low volume; single-source risk |
| Cold chain packaging (dry ice, PCM) | Road (local supply) | Must be sourced near point of pack |

## Distribution Norms
- Strictly regulated: GDP (Good Distribution Practice) mandatory throughout chain
- Temperature-controlled lanes: 2–8°C for biologics and vaccines; ambient for tablets; ultra-cold (-60 to -80°C) for mRNA
- Hub-and-spoke through qualified wholesalers (QWP): pharma cannot typically deliver direct to pharmacy in most EU markets
- Direct-to-hospital (DTH): for high-value oncology, biologics, and infusion products
- Serialisation / track-and-trace: mandatory — EU FMD (Falsified Medicines Directive), US DSCSA (Drug Supply Chain Security Act)
- Patient support programmes: biologics increasingly delivered at home with nurse support; complex logistics

## Outbound Modal Split
| Channel | Mode | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Wholesale distributor (QWP) | Road FTL (GDP-compliant) | Bulk ambient distribution |
| Hospital / institutional (DTH) | Road LTL or validated courier | Temperature-controlled; often cold chain |
| Pharmacy (retail) | Road LTL | Mixed product; ambient or chilled |
| Export (developing markets) | Ocean FCL or Air | Value and temperature determine mode |
| Cold chain (biologics / GLP-1) | Air (long-haul) or validated road | Time + temperature critical |
| Patient / home delivery | Parcel (GDP-compliant, cold) | Specialty pharmacy; home infusion |
| Clinical trials / IMP | Air or specialist courier | Investigational medicinal product; complex |

## Air Freight Use Cases (structurally higher than other sectors — patient safety)
- Cold chain biologics: GLP-1 (Ozempic/Wegovy), mAbs — always air for intercontinental
- Launch supply: approval-to-market window very tight; pre-positioned stock flies before approval
- Emergency supply: shortage management; healthcare system demand cannot be deferred
- Clinical trial IMP: small quantities to multiple sites globally; always air
- Recall replacement: FSCA requires rapid replacement of defective product

## Top 10 Use Cases (Business-Driven)
1. **Gross Margin in Commodity Volatility** — Keep gross margin stable within ±1% despite API cost and cold chain logistics cost fluctuation
2. **Launch on Time** — Deliver regulatory approval-to-first-shipment within <4 weeks through pre-positioned supply and pre-approved logistics lanes
3. **Working Capital on Biologic Inventory** — Reduce finished goods inventory days for biologics by 20% without increasing stockout risk
4. **Revenue at Risk from Supply Shortage** — Keep revenue at risk from API or CDMO supply disruption below 2% of quarterly revenue
5. **Model & React to Manufacturing Disruption** — Re-allocate batch production across sites within 48 hours of primary site failure
6. **Cold Chain Compliance** — Achieve 100% GDP temperature compliance across all lanes with real-time monitoring and deviation alerting
7. **Serialisation & Traceability** — Achieve 100% DSCSA and FMD compliance across all US and EU markets with zero regulatory findings
8. **Clinical Supply on Schedule** — Deliver IMP (investigational medicinal product) to all clinical trial sites within <1-week variance of protocol-required date
9. **Demand Spike Absorption (GLP-1)** — Absorb 40% above-forecast demand for blockbuster products without patient treatment gaps or grey market diversion
10. **Generic Entry Defence** — Protect brand revenue by maintaining >98% supply reliability and patient loyalty during first-wave generic entry

## Regulatory & Compliance Considerations
- GDP (Good Distribution Practice): EU Guidelines 2013/C 343/01; WHO GDP — mandatory for all distribution
- GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice): FDA 21 CFR Parts 210/211; EU GMP guidelines — manufacturing standards
- Serialisation: US DSCSA (Drug Supply Chain Security Act); EU FMD (Falsified Medicines Directive); China 2019 track-and-trace
- Narcotic / controlled substances: DEA Schedule (US); national licences per country — strict storage and distribution controls
- Import licences: country-specific drug registration required before any distribution; variable regulatory timelines (6 months to 5+ years for EM)
- IDMP / ISO IDMP (Identification of Medicinal Products): EU regulatory data requirements impacting supply chain master data
- Pharmacovigilance: adverse event reporting requirements affect reverse logistics and returns handling

## Sustainability Signals to Watch For
- Manufacturing energy intensity (kWh per kg API or per batch)
- Cold chain refrigerant transition (F-gas phase-out; natural refrigerant adoption)
- API manufacturing water usage and wastewater treatment (APIs often synthesised in water-intensive processes)
- Packaging reduction and primary packaging sustainability (glass vs plastic vials)
- Scope 3 Category 1 (CDMO manufacturing is largest Scope 3 for many pharma companies)
- Access to medicine programmes in low-income countries (tiered pricing supply chain complexity)
