# Technology Devices — Sector Reference for Supply Chain Mapping

**Cluster:** High Tech
**Representative Companies:** Dell, Logitech, Google (hardware), Apple, Samsung, HP, Lenovo
**Taxonomy description:** Complicated digital things, sold to businesses & consumers

---

## Typical Make/Buy Split
- Consumer electronics: 80–95% outsourced to EMS (Foxconn, Pegatron, Flex, Jabil, Wistron)
- Enterprise hardware: 60–80% EMS + own final assembly / configuration centres
- Peripherals (Logitech model): 90–100% outsourced, company owns only design + firmware
- Key signal: "EMS" or "ODM" partners in 10-K; "contract manufacturer" in risk factors
- China+1 shift accelerating: Vietnam, India, Mexico absorbing production moves post-2022

## Inbound Raw Materials — Typical Modal Split
| Material | Mode | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Semiconductors / ICs | Air | High value-density, time-sensitive, shortage-prone |
| PCBs | Air or Ocean FCL | Depends on lead time buffer and unit value |
| Displays / screens | Ocean FCL | High volume, primarily Korea / China |
| Batteries (Li-ion) | Ocean FCL (IMO Class 9) | Dangerous goods — extra compliance cost |
| Rare earths / metals | Ocean FCL | Long-haul from extraction sites (DRC, China) |
| Plastic / metal casings | Ocean FCL or Road | Typically Asia-origin |
| Packaging materials | Road FTL (local) | Regional supplier preferred |

## Distribution Norms
- Forward-stocking locations (FSLs) near major demand centres (US, DE, SG, JP)
- Merge-in-transit for configure-to-order (CTO) products: components ship separately, assembled at DC
- Significant reverse logistics (10–15% for consumer electronics; 5–8% for enterprise)
- Refurbishment centres typically Eastern Europe (CZ, PL) or SE Asia (MY, TH)
- Carrier/telco channel: device bundled with contract — telco DC receives bulk, kits per order
- B2B/enterprise: often drop-shipped direct to customer site from regional DC

## Outbound Modal Split
| Channel | Mode | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Retail (big box, CE chains) | Ocean FCL + Road FTL | Seasonal build (Q3 ship for Q4) |
| B2B / enterprise | Road LTL or Air | Short customer lead time; high unit value |
| Ecom / DTC | Parcel express | Dominant and growing; same/next-day expectation |
| Carrier / telco | Road FTL | Bundled device + accessory kits |
| Export (EM markets) | Ocean FCL | Grey market risk — serialisation important |

## Air Freight Use Cases (declining for consumer; structural for enterprise)
- New product launch: must hit retail shelves globally on same day — ocean can't match timing
- Semiconductor allocation crises: short allocations flown to avoid production line stoppage
- Enterprise SLA: customer uptime SLA requires next-business-day part delivery
- Refurbishment parts: high-value spare parts for field service

## Top 10 Use Cases (Business-Driven)
1. **Demand Spike Absorption** — Absorb a 30% demand spike at product launch without stockouts or unplanned air freight
2. **Component Shortage Continuity** — Maintain production output during semiconductor allocation crisis with <5% line stoppage
3. **New Product Launch on Time** — Launch new device globally within <2-week variance across all markets simultaneously
4. **Gross Margin Protection** — Keep product gross margin stable within ±2% despite BOM component cost volatility
5. **Inventory Working Capital** — Reduce finished goods inventory days by 15% without degrading service fill rate
6. **Revenue at Risk** — Keep revenue at risk from single-source component exposure below 3% of quarterly revenue
7. **Air Freight Reduction** — Cut air freight spend by 25% YoY through better demand sensing and lead time buffering
8. **China+1 Resilience** — Model and execute production shift to Vietnam/India within 90 days of geopolitical trigger
9. **Returns Value Recovery** — Recover >85% of value from returned/end-of-life devices through refurb and resale programmes
10. **Supplier Diversification** — Reduce single-source exposure across top-20 BOM components by 40% within 18 months

## Regulatory & Compliance Considerations
- Export controls: EAR (US), ITAR where applicable, EU dual-use regulations — critical for enterprise/gov products
- Battery transport: IATA DGR Section II for Li-ion; IMO Class 9 for ocean
- RoHS / WEEE: restricts hazardous substances; drives reverse logistics programme requirements
- Conflict minerals (3TG): SEC Rule 13p-1 / EU conflict minerals regulation
- GDPR / data privacy: devices with personal data require secure data erasure in reverse logistics

## Sustainability Signals to Watch For
- Air freight % of total shipments (vs prior year baseline)
- Packaging recyclability and reduction commitments
- Product carbon footprint (PCF) per device
- Refurbishment / circular economy volume targets
- Supplier renewable energy % (Scope 3 Category 1)
